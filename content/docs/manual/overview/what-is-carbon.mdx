Carbon is software for running a manufacturing business. It tracks your orders, parts,
materials, and money in one place. Write something down once, and Carbon carries it
everywhere. Nobody re-types anything.

Most shops glue this together from spreadsheets, an accounting program, paper travelers,
and inspection forms. None of them talk to each other. Carbon replaces that pile with one
**system of record** — one trusted place where the real state of the business lives.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Carbon is organized into **Modules** — Sales, Purchasing, Items, Production, Inventory,
Quality, Accounting, and more. Each Module is one job. You open a Module from the **home
screen**, where every Module shows up as a tile.

## ERP, MES and QMS — three jobs, one system

People describe business software with three acronyms — ERP, MES, and QMS. Many shops buy
three separate programs. Carbon does all three — not bolted together, but as three views of
the **same data**.

### ERP — the office

**ERP** means *Enterprise Resource Planning*. In plain words, it's the office side. The
Modules here let you:

- **Quote** a customer, then turn the quote into a **sales order** when they say yes.
- Raise **purchase orders** to suppliers for the materials you need.
- Track **inventory** — what's on the shelf, where, and how much.
- Roll up **cost** automatically, so you know if a price leaves a profit.

For Acme, ERP took Radiant Aerospace's order for 500 Counterweight Hooks (sales order
`SO000069`, $36,820) and bought the steel from Ryerson (purchase order `PO000057`).

### MES — the shop floor

**MES** means *Manufacturing Execution System*. It's what operators use on the floor. When
the office releases a **job** (an order to make a quantity of a part), it shows up for an
operator. They open it, **clock on** to an operation, and record finished pieces.

For Acme's hook, the floor ran three steps in order — **Flame Cut**, **Sandblasting**, then
**Inspection** — all on job `J000097`.

### QMS — quality

**QMS** means *Quality Management System*. It's how you make sure each part is right and
can prove it later:

- **Inspections** — a quality check, often built into a job as a step.
- **Non-conformances (NCRs)** — a record you raise when something is wrong, so it gets fixed.
- **Traceability** — the linked chain from finished part back to the exact batch of raw
  material. Carbon can answer "which heat of steel went into Radiant's hooks?" in seconds.

Because it's one system, hand-offs are automatic. The estimator doesn't email the order to
the floor — the order *becomes* a job. The inspector doesn't keep a separate binder — the
inspection is a step on that same job.

## One database underneath

The three sides feel like one tool because they sit on **one database**. A sales order, the
job that fulfills it, the purchase order for its material, the inspection that passed it,
and the invoice that bills it are all linked records.

## Meet Acme — our running example

Every page in this manual uses one real demo company so the examples stay concrete.
**Acme Inc.** is a metal-fabrication shop in Springfield, OH. It cuts, machines, and
finishes steel and aluminum parts for aerospace, defense, energy, mining, and construction.

## What makes Carbon different

<Tabs items={["One system", "API-first", "AI-native", "Open source"]}>
  <Tab value="One system">
    Everything lives together, so there's **no re-keying** and nothing drifts out of sync.
    A quote becomes a sales order becomes a job becomes a shipment becomes an invoice — each
    inheriting from the last. The [walkthrough](/manual/walkthrough) follows that chain for
    Acme's hook, from price request to paid invoice.
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="API-first">
    An **API** lets other software read and write your data. Carbon is *API-first*: every
    screen you click does the same thing a program could do through the API. So you can
    connect Carbon to your other tools and automate the boring parts. More in
    [Using the API](/manual/developing/using-the-api).
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="AI-native">
    Carbon ships with a built-in **MCP server** — a safe doorway that lets an AI assistant
    operate Carbon for you (look things up, draft a quote, check a job) in plain language.
    The demo data in this manual was pulled through it. See
    [MCP & AI](/manual/developing/mcp-and-ai).
  </Tab>
  <Tab value="Open source">
    Carbon's code is **open source** — anyone can read it, run it, and extend it. You can
    self-host, audit how it works, and shape it to your shop. More in
    [Integrating with Carbon](/manual/developing/integrating).
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout type="tip">
  You don't need the API or AI features to get full value from Carbon. Day one, you can
  quote, order, make, buy, inspect and invoice entirely by clicking. New to a bolded term?
  Every one is defined in the [glossary](/manual/overview/glossary).
</Callout>

## The one idea to remember

**Carbon is one connected system of record for the whole business.** ERP runs the office,
MES runs the floor, QMS keeps it good — three views into the same data, organized as
Modules. Information goes in once and follows the work from the first quote to the final
payment.
