The first time you open Carbon there's a lot on screen — a column of icons down the left,
a search box up top, and a big table in the middle. The good news: the whole app is built
from a handful of repeating pieces. Learn those pieces once and every screen, from a
purchase order to a quality inspection, suddenly feels familiar. This page is the guided
tour: what's where, how to jump anywhere in two keystrokes, and how to read the two screens
you'll see most — the **list** and the **record**.

<Callout type="info">
  **Before you start.** This page assumes you can sign in to Carbon (or you're following
  along with the live demo). If you haven't yet, your administrator sets you up in
  [Users & permissions](/manual/getting-started/users-permissions). New to the *ideas*
  behind Carbon — items, jobs, orders? Read [Core concepts](/manual/overview/core-concepts)
  alongside this page; here we focus only on driving the interface.
</Callout>

## The big picture

Every page in Carbon shares the same frame. A **module rail** runs down the left edge — that's
how you switch between big areas of the business like Sales, Purchasing or Production. Across
the top sits the **search bar** (your fastest way to jump anywhere) plus your account and
notifications. Everything else — the list, the record you opened, the form you're filling in —
loads in the large **content area** in the middle.

## Jump anywhere with search (⌘K)

The single most useful habit in Carbon is the **command palette**. Press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> (on
Windows, <kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>K</kbd>) — or click the search bar at the top — and a search
box opens over whatever you're doing. Start typing and Carbon searches everything at once:
records by number, parts by name, and the app's own navigation.

Type a record number you already know — `SO000069`, `J000097`, `Q000061` — and press
<kbd>Enter</kbd> to land straight on it. Type a part name like *Counterweight Hook* and you'll
get the item plus every order and job that touches it. You can also search for screens
themselves: typing *Suppliers* or *Work Centers* jumps you to that list without ever touching
the rail.

<Callout type="tip">
  ⌘K is faster than clicking once you trust it. Think of it the way you'd use a browser's
  address bar: don't navigate to a thing — just name it.
</Callout>

## Lists vs records: the two screens you'll always see

Almost everything you open in Carbon is one of two screens. Understanding the difference is
most of what "knowing the app" means.

- A **list** (also called a *table* or *index*) shows *many* records of one kind — every job,
  every customer, every purchase order — one per row. You use it to find, filter and scan.
- A **record** (also called a *detail* view) shows *one* of those records in full, with all
  its fields, tabs and related documents. You use it to read or edit a single thing.

You move between them constantly: open a list, click a row, and you're in that record. The
flow below is the same everywhere in Carbon.

### The list view

A list is a table. Every row is one record; the columns are its most important fields. At the
top you'll find a **search/filter** bar to narrow the rows, and a **New** button to create one.
Status columns use color-coded [badges](/manual/overview/glossary) so you can scan the health
of dozens of records at a glance.

A few things to know about every list in Carbon:

- **Click a row** to open that record. The row number, like `SO000069`, is also a clickable
  link.
- **Search and filter** at the top narrow the rows — by status, customer, date, and more.
- **Sort** by clicking a column header.
- **Columns are yours to arrange.** You can show, hide and reorder columns, and Carbon
  remembers your choice for that list.

### The record view

Click `SO000069` and you land on its **record**. A record gathers everything about one thing:
its key fields up top, a **status** badge showing where it stands, and a set of **tabs** that
group related information — and related documents, like the lines on an order or the
shipments against it.

The **tabs** are the key to a record. On this sales order, *Details* holds the header you see
above; *Lines* holds the parts ordered; *Shipments* and *Invoices* hold the documents created
from it. Different record types have different tabs — a Job has *Operations* and *Materials*,
a Part has *Manufacturing* and *Costing* — but the pattern is always the same: header on top,
related detail in tabs.

<Callout type="tip">
  Records are **linked**. From SO000069 you can hop straight to the job that makes it
  (J000097), the shipment that sent it, or the invoice that billed it (AR000025) — usually
  by clicking the linked number. This is the heart of how Carbon works: one continuous chain
  of records, each a click from the next. You can watch the whole chain unfold in the
  [15-minute walkthrough](/manual/walkthrough).
</Callout>

## Reading status badges

Carbon uses small colored **status badges** everywhere — in lists, on records, in search.
They're shorthand for "where is this in its lifecycle?" The colors are consistent across the
app, so once you learn them you can read any screen at a glance:

<DataTable
  columns={[
    { key: "badge", label: "Looks like" },
    { key: "means", label: "Roughly means" },
    { key: "eg", label: "Example" },
  ]}
  rows={[
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="neutral">Draft</StatusBadge>, means: "Started, not yet finalized", eg: "A quote you're still building" },
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="blue">Ready</StatusBadge>, means: "Open / awaiting action", eg: "A job ready for the floor" },
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="amber">In Progress</StatusBadge>, means: "Underway right now", eg: "A job being worked" },
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="green">Completed</StatusBadge>, means: "Done / active / paid", eg: "SO000069, AR000025" },
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="purple">On Hold</StatusBadge>, means: "Paused on purpose", eg: "A customer on credit hold" },
    { badge: <StatusBadge tone="red">Cancelled</StatusBadge>, means: "Stopped / rejected", eg: "A cancelled order" },
  ]}
/>

<Callout type="info">
  Each record type has its own exact set of statuses — a purchase order moves through *Draft →
  Needs Approval → To Receive → To Invoice*, for instance, while a quote moves *Draft → Sent →
  Accepted*. The **colors** stay consistent even when the words differ, so amber always means
  "in motion" and green always means "good / done".
</Callout>

## Creating a new record

Wherever there's a list, there's a way to add to it. The pattern is always the same:

<Steps>
  <Step>
    **Go to the right list.** Pick the module in the left rail, then the list you want — for a
    new order, **Sales → Sales Orders**. Or just press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> and type the list name.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Click `New`** (top-right of the list). Carbon opens a blank record — often as a panel that
    slides in — and assigns the next number automatically. You don't pick the number; Carbon
    hands you `SO000072` after `SO000071`.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Fill in the header.** Choose the customer or supplier, the location, dates and terms.
    Required fields are marked; Carbon won't let you save without them.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Save, then add the detail.** Once the header is saved the record opens in full, with its
    tabs. Now add the **lines** — the parts, quantities and prices — under the *Lines* tab.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Advance the status when you're ready.** A new record starts as a <StatusBadge tone="neutral">Draft</StatusBadge>.
    Actions like *Confirm*, *Release* or *Send* (usually a button near the status) move it
    forward and trigger the next steps — like creating a job from a confirmed order.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">
  Many records start in **Draft** and do nothing until you advance them. A purchase order sitting
  in <StatusBadge tone="neutral">Draft</StatusBadge> hasn't been sent to the supplier; a quote in
  Draft hasn't reached the customer. If something "didn't happen," check its status first — it's
  the most common surprise for new users.
</Callout>
