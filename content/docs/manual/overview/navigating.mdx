Carbon looks busy at first, but it's built from a few repeating pieces. Learn them once and
every screen feels familiar. This is the quick tour.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Carbon is split into **Modules** — Sales, Purchasing, Items, Production, Inventory, Quality,
and more. Each Module is one area of your business. When you open Carbon you land on the
**home screen**, where every Module shows up as a tile.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/home-modules.png"
  alt="The Carbon home screen with a tile for each module"
  caption="The home screen. Each tile is a Module — click one to go there. The same Modules also sit in the rail down the left edge."
/>

Two ways to move around:

- **Module tiles** on the home screen.
- The **module rail** — the column of icons down the left edge, on every page.

<Callout type="tip">
  You don't need every Module on day one. Most people live in one or two. What you can see also
  depends on your [permissions](/manual/getting-started/users-permissions), so your rail may be
  shorter than the screenshots here.
</Callout>

## What each Module is for

| Module | What you do here |
| --- | --- |
| **Sales** | Quote, sell and ship to customers |
| **Purchasing** | Buy materials and services from suppliers |
| **Items** | Define the parts and materials you make and buy |
| **Production** | Plan and run jobs on the shop floor |
| **Inventory** | Track stock across locations |
| **Quality** | Inspect work and log issues |
| **Accounting** | Invoice, get paid and post to the books |
| **Settings** | Set up your company and account |

## Open a Module

Click a Module tile (or its icon in the rail) and Carbon opens that area. Below, clicking
**Items** opens the Items Module and its **Parts** list.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/nav-items.gif"
  alt="Clicking the Items module tile opens the Parts list"
  caption="Click a Module to open it. The frame never moves — only the middle of the screen changes."
/>

The outer frame stays put. Only the **content area** in the middle swaps to show the Module
you picked.

## Jump anywhere with search

The fastest way to get around is search. Press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> (<kbd>Ctrl</kbd>+<kbd>K</kbd> on
Windows), or click the search bar up top, and a box opens over whatever you're doing.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/search-palette.png"
  alt="The Carbon command palette open, searching across the app"
  caption="Press ⌘K and type. Carbon searches records, parts, and screens all at once."
/>

You can search three ways:

- **A record number** you already know — type `SO000069` and press <kbd>Enter</kbd> to land on it.
- **A part name** — type *Counterweight Hook* to find the item plus the orders and jobs that use it.
- **A screen** — type *Suppliers* or *Work Centers* to jump straight to that list.

<Callout type="tip">
  Think of <kbd>⌘K</kbd> like a browser address bar: don't navigate to a thing — just name it.
</Callout>

## The two screens you'll always see

Almost everything in Carbon is one of two screens. Knowing the difference is most of "knowing
the app."

### List view

A **list** shows *many* records of one kind — every order, every part — one per row. Use it to
find, filter and scan.

- **Click a row** to open that record.
- **Search and filter** at the top to narrow the rows.
- **Sort** by clicking a column header.
- **New** button (top-right) creates a record.

### Detail view

A **record** (or *detail*) shows *one* thing in full — all its fields, a **status** badge, and
**tabs** that group related info. On a sales order like `SO000069`, the *Lines* tab holds the
parts ordered, *Shipments* holds what was sent, *Invoices* holds the bills.

<Callout type="tip">
  Records are **linked**. From a sales order you can hop to the job that makes it, the shipment
  that sent it, and the invoice that billed it — usually by clicking the linked number. Watch the
  whole chain in the [15-minute walkthrough](/manual/walkthrough).
</Callout>

## Lost? Two quick resets

- Press <kbd>⌘K</kbd> to search from anywhere.
- Click the **Carbon logo** (top-left) to return home.

You can't get truly stuck — every record is reachable by its number.
