---
title: "Carbon in 15 minutes"
description: "Follow one real order from quote to cash — and see how Carbon's modules connect."
---

The fastest way to "get" Carbon is to follow one order through it. We'll track a single real
part — a **Counterweight Hook** that Acme makes for **Radiant Aerospace** — from the first
price request to a paid invoice. You'll touch most of the Modules along the way.

<Callout type="info">
  **The part:** the *Counterweight Hook* (part number `CWH-A36-15`) is a steel hook cut from
  1-inch plate. Radiant orders 500 at a time. You don't need to do anything here — just read.
</Callout>

Here's the whole journey. Each step happens in a different Module, and Carbon links them all
together automatically:

<Mermaid
  chart={`flowchart LR
  Q[Quote] --> SO[Sales Order]
  SO --> J[Job]
  J --> PO[Purchase Order]
  PO --> J
  J --> SH[Shipment]
  SH --> INV[Invoice]`}
/>

## Step 1 — Quote the customer · *Sales module*

Radiant asks "what do 500 hooks cost?" You answer with a **quote**. Open the **Sales** module,
go to **Quotes**, and you'll see every quote — here's `Q000061` for Radiant, already marked
**Ordered** (they said yes).

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-quotes.png"
  alt="The Quotes list in the Sales module"
  caption="Sales → Quotes. Click the green Add Quote button to start one, or a row to open it."
/>

You add a line for the part and quantity; Carbon adds up the material and shop time so you
can set a price. When the customer says yes, one click turns the quote into a **sales order**.

## Step 2 — Take the order · *Sales module*

The **sales order** is the customer's commitment. In **Sales → Orders** you can see `SO000069`
for Radiant Aerospace — **Completed**, **$36,820**.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-orders.png"
  alt="The Sales Orders list showing SO000069 for Radiant Aerospace"
  caption="Sales → Orders. The colored pills are statuses: Draft, Confirmed, In Progress, Completed."
/>

Open the order and you see the line: **CWH-A36-15**, 500 each at **$73.14**. Notice it's set to
**Make to Order** — so Carbon knows it has to be *built*, and it already created a job for it.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-order-detail.png"
  alt="Sales order SO000069 detail, showing the line item and totals"
  caption="The order links straight to its job, shipments and invoices (buttons top-right)."
/>

## Step 3 — How it's made · *Items module*

Every part you make has a **Method** — a recipe with two halves: the **Bill of Material** (what
it's made of) and the **routing** (the steps to make it). Open the part in the **Items** module
to see it.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/item-method.png"
  alt="The Counterweight Hook's manufacturing method: bill of material and 3D model"
  caption="The hook's method: it's made from 40 lb of A36 Steel Plate. Replenishment = Make."
/>

Each hook needs **40 lb** of steel, so 500 hooks need **20,000 lb**. Carbon already knows that —
watch what it does next.

## Step 4 — Make it · *Production module*

Because the order is *Make to Order*, Carbon creates a **job** (`J000097`) for 500 pieces. The
**Production** module is mission control for the shop — here you can see active jobs and how busy
each work center is.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/production-dashboard.png"
  alt="The Production module dashboard with active jobs and work center utilization"
  caption="Production → the job runs three steps: Flame Cut → Sandblasting → Inspection."
/>

On the shop floor, operators open the **Shop Floor** app, tap onto each step, and record how
many good pieces they finished. That's how the job gets marked done — and how its real cost
adds up.

## Step 5 — Buy the steel · *Purchasing module*

Acme is short on 1-inch plate, so Carbon flags it and you raise a **purchase order** to your
steel supplier, **Ryerson** (`PO000057`). The **Purchasing** module tracks all of this.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/purchasing-dashboard.png"
  alt="The Purchasing module dashboard with open purchase orders"
  caption="Purchasing → Orders. When the steel arrives, a quick Receipt adds it to inventory."
/>

Because A36 plate is **batch-tracked**, Carbon records exactly which steel went into this batch
of hooks — the start of full [traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality).

## Step 6 — Check it and ship it · *Quality + Inventory*

The job's last step is **Inspection** — a quality gate. Once the parts pass, you create a
**shipment** and send the 500 hooks. Carbon marks the order line **Shipped**.

<StatGrid cols={3}>
  <Stat label="Made" value="500" hint="all steps done" />
  <Stat label="Shipped" value="500" hint="to Radiant Aerospace" />
  <Stat label="On time" value="Yes" hint="before the due date" />
</StatGrid>

## Step 7 — Get paid · *Accounting module*

Finally you bill the order. The **sales invoice** (`AR000025`) pulls its lines from what
shipped — no re-typing — and once Radiant pays, it's marked **Paid**.

<StatGrid cols={3}>
  <Stat label="Subtotal" value="$36,570" />
  <Stat label="Shipping" value="$250" />
  <Stat label="Invoice total" value="$36,820" hint="Paid" />
</StatGrid>
