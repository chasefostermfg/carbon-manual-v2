The fastest way to understand Carbon is to watch one order move through it. This page
follows a single real part — a **Counterweight Hook** that Acme makes for **Radiant
Aerospace** — from the first price request all the way to a paid invoice. You don't need
to *do* anything here; just read along and you'll have the whole map. Every later section
zooms in on one of these stops.

  chart={`flowchart LR

  SO --> J[Job J000097]
  J --> PO[Purchase Order PO000057]
  PO --> J
  J --> SH[Shipment]
  SH --> INV[Invoice AR000025]`}
/>

<Callout type="info">
  **Meet the part.** The *Counterweight Hook* (part number `CWH-A36-15`) is a heavy steel
  hook flame-cut from 1-inch A36 steel plate. Radiant Aerospace orders them 500 at a time.
  Carbon calls anything you make or buy an [**item**](/manual/overview/glossary) — this one
  is a *Part* that Acme **makes** (rather than buys).
</Callout>

## Step 1 — A customer asks for a price (the Quote)

It starts when Radiant Aerospace asks "what would 500 of these cost?" In Carbon you answer
with a **quote**: you add a line for the part, the quantity, and a price. Carbon adds up the
cost of materials and shop time behind the scenes so you can price the work with confidence.

When the customer says yes, you **convert the quote into a sales order** with one click —
no re-typing. Carbon keeps the quote and the order linked, so you can always trace a job
back to the price you promised.

<Callout type="tip">
  Quotes are also where Carbon's **configurators** shine — for made-to-order parts the
  customer's choices (size, material, finish) can drive the price automatically. More in
  [Quoting & estimating](/manual/selling/quoting).
</Callout>

## Step 2 — You win the work (the Sales Order)

The **sales order** is the customer's commitment: who's buying, what, how many, when it's
due, and at what price. It's the anchor everything else hangs off — the job that makes the
part, the shipment that sends it, and the invoice that bills for it all point back here.

Notice the **fulfillment method** on the line: *Make to Order*. That single setting tells
Carbon this part isn't sitting on a shelf — it has to be **made** — so the next step happens
almost automatically.

## Step 3 — Carbon knows how it's made (the Method)

Every part you make has a **method**: a recipe with two halves.

- The **bill of materials (BOM)** — what it's made *of*.
- The **routing** — the steps to make it, and where each step happens.

For the Counterweight Hook, the recipe is short: take a slab of 1-inch A36 steel plate,
then run it through three shop operations.

Each hook needs **40 lb** of steel, so a batch of 500 needs **20,000 lb**. Carbon already
knows that — and it knows Acme doesn't keep that much on hand. Watch what happens next.

## Step 4 — Carbon plans the work (the Job)

Because the order is *Make to Order*, Carbon creates a **job** — a production order that
carries its own copy of the method. Acme **releases** job `J000097` for 500 pieces and it's
ready for the shop floor.

On the shop floor, operators open the [**MES app**](/manual/manufacturing/shop-floor),
clock onto each operation, and record how many good pieces they finished. That's how the
"Done" statuses above got there — and how the 282 seconds of recorded labor rolled up into
the job's real cost.

## Step 5 — Buy the material you don't have (the Purchase Order)

Acme is short on 1-inch steel plate, so Carbon flags the shortage and Acme raises a
**purchase order** to its steel supplier, **Ryerson**. Buying and making run in parallel —
the steel is on its way while the shop gets set up.

When the steel arrives, a quick **receipt** adds it to inventory — and because A36 plate is
**batch-tracked**, Carbon records exactly which heat of steel went into this batch of hooks.
That's the start of full [traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality): months later you
can answer "which material went into Radiant's order?" in seconds.

## Step 6 — Check it, ship it (Quality & Shipment)

The last operation on the job was **Inspection** — a quality gate. With the parts inspected
and accepted, Acme creates a **shipment**, picks the 500 finished hooks, and sends them.
Carbon marks the sales order line *Shipped*.

## Step 7 — Get paid (the Invoice)

Finally, Acme bills the order. The **sales invoice** `AR000025` pulls its lines straight
from what shipped — no re-keying — and once Radiant pays, Carbon marks it **Paid** and posts
the revenue to the books.
