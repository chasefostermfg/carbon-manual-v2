title: "Replenishment: make vs. buy"
description: "Reorder policies, lead times and planning parameters that keep stock flowing."
---

Every [item](/manual/overview/glossary) has to come from somewhere. You either **make** it
or **buy** it. **Replenishment** is how you tell Carbon which one — and the few numbers that
let Carbon top up your stock before you run out.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Replenishment lives in two **Modules**:

- **Items** — set **Make** or **Buy** on the item, in the **Replenishment** area.
- **Inventory** — see the reorder policy and stock levels Carbon uses to plan.

Open an item from the **Items** module and look at its **Manufacturing** view. The
**Replenishment** field is the make-or-buy switch.

## Make or buy

Pick one of these on every item:

- **Make** — Carbon builds it on a [job](/manual/overview/glossary). It needs a
  [method](/manual/items/methods) (a BOM + routing). The Counterweight Hook **CWH-A36-15**
  is **Make**.
- **Buy** — Carbon orders it from a supplier on a [purchase order](/manual/overview/glossary).
  It needs a supplier and a price. The A36 steel plate it's cut from is **Buy**.
- **Make & Buy** — both are allowed. You pick make-or-buy on each order line.

<Callout type="tip">
  Rule of thumb: if an item has a **method**, it's **Make**. If it has a **supplier** and a
  purchase price, it's **Buy**.
</Callout>

## How a line gets filled

When an item is ordered (a sales order, or a part needed by a job), Carbon picks how to fill
that one line:

- **Make to Order** — start a fresh job just for this order. Best for custom parts you don't stock.
- **Make to Stock** — build a batch into inventory ahead of demand, then ship from the shelf.
- **Pull from Inventory** — take what's already on the shelf. No new job or PO.

On Acme's hook order, the line is **Make to Order**, so Carbon created job **J000097**. Inside
that job, the A36 steel is set to **Pull from Inventory** — Carbon draws it from the shelf
instead of buying fresh for the job.

<Callout type="info">
  If the shelf is short when Carbon goes to pull, that shortage becomes new demand. Because
  steel is a **Buy** item, that turns into a suggested purchase order — exactly how
  **PO000057** to **Ryerson** came to exist.
</Callout>

## Plan when to reorder

For pure make-to-order parts, planning is simple: an order arrives, a job runs. The numbers
below matter most for items you **stock** — bulk raw materials and make-to-stock parts.

Open the **Inventory** module to see the reorder policy and stock levels Carbon plans against,
item by item.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/inventory.png"
  alt="The Inventory module showing on-hand quantities, reorder policy and replenishment by item"
  caption="Inventory module. Each item shows its reorder policy and replenishment columns Carbon uses to suggest top-ups."
/>

Set these on the item's **Planning** tab, **per location** (Headquarters and Texas can differ):

<Steps>

<Step>
**Pick a reordering policy** — when Carbon suggests a top-up:
- **Manual Reorder** — no auto-suggestions; you create jobs and POs yourself.
- **Demand-Based Reorder** — Carbon adds up demand over a window (Acme uses **4 weeks**) and
  suggests enough to cover it.
- **Maximum Quantity** — keep the shelf topped up toward a target level.
</Step>

<Step>
**Set the reorder point and quantity.** Drop below the **reorder point** and Carbon raises a
suggestion. The **reorder quantity** is how much to order each time.
</Step>

<Step>
**Set safety stock.** A buffer kept on the shelf beyond known demand. Acme keeps **3,000 LB**
of A36 steel at Headquarters. The hook keeps **none** — it's made to order.
</Step>

<Step>
**Set the lead time.** How long replenishment takes. Both hero items use **7 days**. Carbon
works back from the due date to tell you the latest day to order or release.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">
  Reorder points are **per location**, not per company. If you stock the same material in two
  places, give each location its own numbers — or one site runs dry while the other overflows.
</Callout>
