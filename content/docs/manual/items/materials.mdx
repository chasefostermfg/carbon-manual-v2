A **material** is the raw stock you buy and then cut, machine or form into parts — a steel
plate, an aluminum billet, a length of brass bar. In Carbon, a material is a kind of
[**item**](/manual/overview/glossary), described the way a metals distributor sells it:
a **substance**, a **form**, a **grade**, and **dimensions**.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Materials live in the **Items** module, on the **Materials** tab next to Parts. Open **Items**
from the home screen, then switch to **Materials** to see your raw stock.

## Material vs. part

Both are **items**, so they share the same fields — ID, unit of measure, costs, inventory,
suppliers. They differ in what they describe and how you get them.

## The four parts of a material

Every material is described by four attributes. Acme packs all four into a readable ID so the
catalog reads at a glance.

<StatGrid cols={4}>
  <Stat label="Substance" value="A36" hint="the alloy" />
  <Stat label="Form" value="Plate" hint="the shape" />
  <Stat label="Grade" value="A36" hint="temper / spec" />
  <Stat label="Dimensions" value="1″ · 96×240" hint="thickness · sheet" />
</StatGrid>

- **Substance** — the alloy. Carbon ships 17 built-in (Steel, Stainless Steel, Aluminum,
  Brass, Inconel, Titanium and more). Acme uses six, plus a custom one (*Foam*) for packaging.
- **Form** — the shape it arrives in. Carbon has 21 built-in: Plate, Billet, Flat Bar, Round
  Bar, Hex Bar, Sheet, Tube, Angle, Channel and more.
- **Grade** — the temper or spec, like `T6` on aluminum or `304` vs `316` on stainless.
- **Dimensions** — the physical size: plate thickness, sheet size, bar diameter.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Reading an Acme ID.** `A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240` = **A36** (grade) · **STEEL** (substance) ·
  **PLT** (form) · **1″** (thickness) · **96×240** (sheet, inches).
</Callout>

## Cut-to-size stock and stock on hand

You rarely use a whole sheet. Each job pulls only the **quantity it needs**, in the material's
unit:

- The Counterweight Hook (`CWH-A36-15`) pulls **40 LB** of A36 plate per hook.
- A batch of 500 hooks needs **20,000 LB** — not 500 whole sheets.
- Carbon tracks the running pounds on hand and counts them down as you consume.

To see live stock, open the **Inventory** module. It lists on-hand quantity, reorder policy
and replenishment for every item — including Acme's steel and aluminum.

## Batch tracking and traceability

For materials, **where the metal came from** can matter as much as how much you have. A
**batch** (also *lot*, or *heat* for steel) is stock that arrived together in one shipment.
Acme's A36 plate has batch tracking on, so:

<Steps>
  <Step>
    **At receipt**, Carbon records a batch number when steel arrives from
    [Ryerson](/manual/buying/suppliers) on [PO000057](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics). The
    pounds are tagged with that batch.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **During the job**, when plate is issued to job J000097, Carbon records which batch was used.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Forever after**, each finished hook links back to that exact heat of steel — so if Ryerson
    reports a problem, Acme can answer "which orders shipped that steel?" in seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

This chain — shipment → batch → job → part → customer — is **traceability**. It's why Radiant
Aerospace cares that Acme runs batch-tracked steel. More in
[tracking quality](/manual/quality/tracking-quality).

<Callout type="warn">
  Turn on batch tracking **before** you receive any stock. Switching it on later leaves existing
  quantity with no batch number. For regulated work, enable it when you first create the material.
</Callout>

## Create a material

Here's how Acme added the 1-inch A36 plate. Same steps for steel, stainless, aluminum or brass.

<Steps>
  <Step>
    **Start a new material.** In the **Items** module, on the **Materials** tab, click the green
    **Add** button (top-right). Picking *Material* gives you the substance/form/grade fields.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Pick substance, form and grade.** Choose **Steel** and **Plate (PLT)**, enter grade **A36**,
    and dimensions **1″** thick, **96 × 240** sheet. Carbon builds a readable ID like
    `A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240`.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Set the unit of measure.** Choose **LB (Pound)** — the unit you buy and consume the plate in.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Set replenishment to Buy.** Acme buys plate, so set
    [replenishment](/manual/items/replenishment) to **Buy**. This unlocks supplier and reorder settings.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Turn on batch tracking (if needed).** For traceable stock, switch on **Requires batch
    tracking** now — before any inventory exists.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    **Add the supplier and save.** On the **Suppliers** tab, add **Ryerson** at **$0.25/LB**, then
    **Save**. The material can now drop onto any [method](/manual/items/methods) and onto a
    [purchase order](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics) like PO000057.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  **Reuse, don't duplicate.** Search first — Acme already stocks A36 plate in 1″, 2″, 3″ and 4″.
  Only create a new material when the physical stock is genuinely different.
</Callout>
