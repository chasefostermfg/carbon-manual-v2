A **method** is Carbon's recipe for a part you **make**. It has two halves: what the part is made *of* (the **bill of materials**) and how it's *built* (the **routing**).

Set it once. Every quote, job, and cost estimate is built from it automatically.

This page builds the method for Acme's hero part, the **Counterweight Hook** (`CWH-A36-15`).

## Where this lives in Carbon

Methods live in the **Items** module, on each part's **Manufacturing** tab. Open the part, click **Manufacturing**, and you'll see the whole recipe as a tree: the part on top, its materials and operations nested underneath.

<Callout type="tip">
  The part itself — name, unit, **Make** or **Buy** — is set on the item. See [Defining an item](/manual/items/defining-an-item). The method is where the *making* lives.
</Callout>

## The two halves of a method

- **Bill of materials (BOM)** — *what the part is made of.* A list of materials and sub-parts, each with a quantity per finished piece. The hook needs one line: **40 LB** of A36 steel plate.
- **Routing** — *how the part is made.* An ordered list of [operations](/manual/overview/glossary), each run at a [work center](/manual/overview/glossary) with a time. The hook: flame cut, then sandblast, then inspect.

## Build the bill of materials

The BOM answers one question: *to make one, what do I use?* Enter the quantity for a **single** piece. Carbon scales it later — a job for 500 hooks needs 40 × 500 = **20,000 LB** of plate, automatically.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Open the Manufacturing tab

In the **Items** module, open the Counterweight Hook (`CWH-A36-15`) and click the **Manufacturing** tab.
</Step>

<Step>
### Add a material line

Under **Bill of Material**, click **Add Material**. Pick **A36 Steel Plate 1″** (`A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240`). Carbon fills in the description and unit.
</Step>

<Step>
### Set the quantity per piece

Enter **40** in **LB** (pounds) — the amount one hook uses. Don't multiply by your batch size.
</Step>

<Step>
### Choose the method type

Set the line to **Pull from Inventory**. That tells Carbon to pull the steel off the shelf when a job runs.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  **Pull from Inventory** vs. **Make.** Use **Pull from Inventory** for stocked materials like the steel plate. Use **Make** when the line is itself something you build — that makes it a sub-assembly (see below).
</Callout>

## Build the routing

The routing is the ordered list of shop-floor steps. Each **operation** says *what* you do, *where* (a work center), and *how long* it takes. Steps are numbered in tens (10, 20, 30…) so you can slot one in between later.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Add the first operation

Under **Routing**, click **Add Operation**. Pick the process **Flame Cut** and the work center **Burn Table — PA2**. Carbon assigns it sequence **10**.
</Step>

<Step>
### Enter the times

- **Setup time** — one-time per job. The flame cut has **0** setup.
- **Run time** — per-piece time. Here, **45 Minutes/Piece**.

`Minutes/Piece` scales with quantity; `Total Minutes` stays fixed.
</Step>

<Step>
### Add the other two steps

- **20 · Sandblasting** — *Sand Blasting Room*, **2 Minutes/Piece**.
- **30 · Inspection** — *Inspection Station*, **10 Minutes/Piece**.

Inspection comes last on purpose — it's the quality gate before the hooks can ship.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  **Setup vs. run time.** Run time scales with quantity; setup doesn't. That's why bigger batches usually cost less per piece — and Carbon does the math the moment you set a job quantity.
</Callout>

## Multi-level methods (sub-assemblies)

The hook goes straight from raw material to finished part — a *single-level* method. But many products are built from sub-parts you also make.

Set a BOM line's method type to **Make** instead of **Pull from Inventory**, and it becomes a **sub-assembly** — Carbon nests that child part's whole method underneath. When a job runs, it can build the sub-assemblies first.

<Callout type="info">
  Define a sub-assembly's method once on its own item, then drop it into as many parent BOMs as you like. Update it in one place and every parent picks up the change on its next job.
</Callout>

## Versions and revisions

Parts change, so methods aren't frozen. Carbon keeps history so today's edit doesn't rewrite what you already shipped.

- A **revision** is a named iteration of the part (Rev 0, Rev A…). The hook is at revision **0**.
- A **version** is Carbon's snapshot of a method. A [job](/manual/manufacturing/jobs) takes its **own copy** when it's created. Editing the part's master method later does **not** change a job already in flight.

<Callout type="warn">
  **Editing the master method only affects future jobs.** To change a job that's already running, edit it on that job's method — not the part. This is why job `J000097` built 500 hooks against the method exactly as it stood the day it was released.
</Callout>
