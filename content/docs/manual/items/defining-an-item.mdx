Almost everything in Carbon starts with an **item** — a single, named thing you make, buy,
sell, or use. Every quote, order, job and invoice points back to an item.

This page lives in the **Items** module. It covers the six kinds of item and how to create one.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Items live in the **Items** module. Open it from the left rail, then pick a list:
**Parts**, **Materials**, **Tools**, and so on. Each list has the same green **Add** button,
top-right.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/items-parts.png"
  alt="The Parts list in the Items module, with MAKE/BUY and tracking columns and a green Add Part button"
  caption="Items → Parts. Click any row to open a part, or the green Add Part button (top-right) to create one."
/>

You describe an item **once**. After that, Carbon reuses the same record everywhere — so a
price fix happens in one place.

## The six item types

The first thing you choose is the **type**. It tells Carbon what role the item plays. Acme
uses all six.

You'll use **Part** and **Material** the most. They sit at opposite ends of the same job:

- A **Part** is what you deliver. Acme's **Counterweight Hook** (`CWH-A36-15`) is a Part.
- A **Material** is what you consume to make a Part. The hook is cut from **A36 Steel Plate**
  (`A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240`), which is a Material.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Tool vs Consumable** is just value and durability. A **Tool** (a caliper, a torque wrench)
  lasts years and is worth tracking. A **Consumable** (sandpaper, welding wire) is cheap and
  used up. Ask: "would I notice if one walked off?" If yes, it's probably a Tool.
</Callout>

## Create a Part

Let's create the Counterweight Hook the way Acme did. The flow is the same for any type — you
just pick a different **Type** in step 2.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Open the Parts list

In the left rail, open the **Items** module and choose **Parts**. Click the green
**Add Part** button, top-right.
</Step>

<Step>
### Enter the basics

Fill in the four fields every item needs. Acme enters:

- **Type** — *Part*
- **Part number** — `CWH-A36-15`
- **Description** — Counterweight Hook
- **Unit of measure** — `EA` (Each)

The part number must be unique. Pick a readable scheme and stick to it.
</Step>

<Step>
### Set tracking and Make-or-Buy

- **Tracking** — *Inventory* (Carbon keeps a running on-hand count). Use *Batch* when the lot
  matters, *Serial* for high-value parts, *Non-Inventory* for services.
- **Replenishment** — *Make* or *Buy*. The hook is **Make**: Carbon builds it on a job. The
  steel plate is **Buy**: Carbon orders it on a purchase order.

<Callout type="warn">
  Tracking is hard to change once an item has history, so set it now. When unsure: **Inventory**
  for physical goods, **Non-Inventory** for services.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Save

Click **Save**. Carbon creates the item, marks it **Active**, and opens its detail page.
That's the whole foundation.
</Step>

</Steps>

The detail page groups everything into tabs. The hook is a **Make** part, so its
**Manufacturing** tab holds the method (the recipe) and a 3D model.

  **Manufacturing** and **Purchasing** are mirror images. A **Make** item fills in
  *Manufacturing* and leaves *Purchasing* empty; a **Buy** item is the reverse. The A36 steel
  plate fills in *Purchasing* (Ryerson, $0.25/LB) and has no method.
</Callout>
