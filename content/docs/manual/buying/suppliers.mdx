A **supplier** (or *vendor*) is any company you buy from — a steel mill, a parts
distributor, an anodizer. Add one before you can order from it. This page shows how, using
Acme's real vendors.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Suppliers live in the **Purchasing** module. Open **Purchasing**, then click **Suppliers**
in the sub-nav across the top.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/purchasing-dashboard.png"
  alt="The Purchasing module home screen with the Suppliers sub-nav"
  caption="Purchasing module. Use the sub-nav (Suppliers, Purchase Orders, and more) to move between buying screens."
/>

Set a supplier up once and the rest of buying gets easier. Every [purchase
order](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics) already knows who to email, where to ship from, how
you pay, and the price to expect.

## What a supplier record holds

You don't have to fill it all in at once — a **name** is enough to start. Add the rest as
you go.

Carbon gives every supplier a short ID like `SUP000013`. The friendly name shows
everywhere; the ID keeps it stable even if you rename the company.

Two small fields shape how a supplier behaves:

- **Type** groups and filters the list. It doesn't affect pricing. Define your own types in
  **Settings** — Acme uses five.
- **Status** controls whether you can buy now. **Active** shows everywhere; **Inactive** is
  hidden from new orders but kept for history — that's how you retire a vendor without
  deleting records.

## Create a supplier

Let's add **Ryerson**, Acme's steel mill.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the form

In **Purchasing → Suppliers**, click **New**. Carbon opens a short form. The only required
field is the **Name**.

</Step>

<Step>

### Fill in the core details

Enter the **Name**, pick a **Type**, and confirm the **Currency** (Carbon defaults to your
base currency — USD for Acme). Then click **Save**.

<AppShot title="New Supplier" url="app.carbon.ms/x/supplier/new" caption="Creating the Ryerson supplier record.">
  <FieldGrid cols={2}>
    <Field label="Name" value="Ryerson" />
    <Field label="Supplier type" value="Metals and Raw Materials" />
    <Field label="Currency" value="USD" />
    <Field label="Status" value="Active" />
  </FieldGrid>
</AppShot>

Carbon assigns the next ID (`SUP000013`) and opens the full record. The other tabs now unlock.

</Step>

<Step>

### Add a contact

Open the **Contacts** tab and click **New**. Add a name, email, and phone for the person you
deal with — usually a sales rep.

Mark one as the **purchasing contact**. That's who Carbon emails when you send a purchase
order, so pick the person who takes orders. You can add as many contacts as you like.

</Step>

<Step>

### Add a location

Open the **Locations** tab and click **New** to enter the ship-from or remit-to address. If
a vendor ships from several plants, add one location per site.

<Callout type="tip">
  Most vendors have a single location, so Carbon fills it in automatically on every purchase
  order. You only choose when there's more than one.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>

### Set payment terms

Open the **Payment** tab and pick a default term. **Net 30** means the invoice is due 30 days
after it's issued. Setting a default here means every new order starts with the right terms.

<DataTable
  columns={[
    { key: "term", label: "Payment term" },
    { key: "meaning", label: "What it means" },
  ]}
  rows={[
    { term: "COD", meaning: "Pay when the goods arrive" },
    { term: "Net 15", meaning: "Due 15 days after the invoice date" },
    { term: "Net 30", meaning: "Due 30 days after the invoice date" },
    { term: "Net 60", meaning: "Due 60 days after the invoice date" },
    { term: "2% 10 Net 30", meaning: "2% off if paid within 10 days, else due in 30" },
    { term: "Net EOM 10th", meaning: "Due the 10th of the next month" },
  ]}
/>

Acme buys from Ryerson on **Net 30** — the same terms that carry onto purchase order
`PO000057`.

</Step>

<Step>

### Link supplier part numbers

Your vendor rarely uses *your* part number. A **supplier part** links the two — it stores the
vendor's part number and price for one of your [items](/manual/overview/glossary). The right
number prints on the order and the price fills in automatically.

Add them two ways:

- **From the supplier** → the **Supplier Parts** tab — good for loading a vendor's catalog.
- **From the item** → the item's **Suppliers** tab — good for "who can sell me this?"

With this in place, picking Ryerson on a purchase order line for A36 plate auto-fills the
`$0.25/LB` price Acme negotiated.

</Step>

</Steps>

Now raising a purchase order is mostly point-and-click: pick Ryerson, pick the material, and
Carbon already knows the contact, address, terms, and price.

<Callout type="warn">
  Don't create a duplicate (a separate "Ryerson Inc." next to "Ryerson"). Duplicates split
  your spending across two records and make reports wrong. Search the list first and reuse
  the vendor if it exists.
</Callout>
