When a job needs material you don't have, you **buy** it with a
[**purchase order**](/manual/overview/glossary) (PO) — a document that tells a
[supplier](/manual/buying/suppliers) what you want, how much, the price, and when. This page
shows how to raise a PO, send it, and **receive** the goods onto your shelf.

We'll follow a real Acme example: **PO000057** to **Ryerson** for the **A36 Steel Plate** that
feeds job `J000097`.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Purchasing is its own **module**. Open it from the left rail to see open POs, the amount on
order, and the sub-nav for **Purchase Orders**, **Suppliers**, and supplier quotes.

<Callout type="info">
  **Where this fits.** A [job](/manual/manufacturing/jobs) (or low stock) creates a need →
  you raise a **PO** here → you [**receive**](/manual/inventory/shipping-receiving) the goods,
  which adds them to inventory → the job uses them → the supplier bills you. It's the buy-side
  mirror of the sell-side flow in [Carbon in 15 minutes](/manual/walkthrough).
</Callout>

## What's on a purchase order

A PO has two parts, shown as tabs on the record:

- **Header** (*Details* tab) — the supplier, dates, deliver-to location, currency, and payment
  terms. One supplier per PO.
- **Lines** (*Lines* tab) — one row per thing you're buying: an
  [item](/manual/overview/glossary), a quantity, a unit price, and a **required date**.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Get a price first.** Not sure what something costs? Send a **supplier quote** (RFQ): you
  ask one or more suppliers for a price, then turn the best reply into a PO without re-typing.
  Carbon also remembers a supplier's last price and pre-fills it — Acme's A36 plate runs
  **$0.25/LB** at Ryerson.
</Callout>

## Create the purchase order

<Steps>

<Step>
### Start a new order

In the **Purchasing** module, open **Purchase Orders** and click **New Purchase Order**.
Carbon assigns the next number (Acme's was `PO000057`) and opens it in **Draft** — nothing is
committed yet, so you can edit or delete freely.
</Step>

<Step>
### Pick the supplier

Choose the **supplier**. Acme picks **Ryerson**. Carbon then fills in that supplier's
**currency** (USD), **payment terms** (Net 30), and contact and address for you.

<Callout type="warn">
  No supplier record yet? Create it first — see [Suppliers](/manual/buying/suppliers). A PO
  must point at a real supplier.
</Callout>
</Step>

<Step>
### Set dates and delivery

On the **Details** tab, confirm the **order date** (2026-06-12) and pick the **deliver-to
location**. Acme ships to **Headquarters**. Leave **Net 30** or override it for this order.
</Step>

<Step>
### Add the line

On the **Lines** tab, click **Add Line** and set the type to **Part**. Search for the item —
Acme picks **A36 Steel Plate 1"** (`A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240`). Then enter the three numbers:

- **Quantity** — `54,001 LB`
- **Unit price** — `$0.25` (Carbon pre-fills the last known price)
- **Required date** — `2026-06-21`

Carbon multiplies them into the **extended price**: 54,001 × $0.25 = **$13,500.25**.

*Purchase Order · PO000057 — Lines. One line: 54,001 LB of A36 steel plate at $0.25/LB.*

<Callout type="tip">
  **Shortcut: buy from a job.** Acme didn't type this line by hand. When job `J000097` was
  planned, Carbon saw it needed 20,000 lb of A36 plate it didn't have and surfaced a
  **shortage** — from there you generate the PO with item, quantity, and supplier already
  filled in. See [Jobs](/manual/manufacturing/jobs) and
  [Advanced purchasing](/manual/buying/advanced-purchasing).
</Callout>

## Approve it and send it

A draft commits you to nothing. Two steps make it a real order.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Submit for approval

When the order is ready, **submit it for approval**. Carbon moves it from **Draft** to
**Needs Approval** — large buys can need a manager's sign-off so nobody accidentally commits
the company. Who approves what is set in [Users & permissions](/manual/getting-started/users-permissions).

A PO walks through these statuses:

/>
</Step>

<Step>
### Approve

An approver opens the order, checks the lines and total, and **approves** it. If something's
wrong, they reject it back to Draft.
</Step>

<Step>
### Send it

Once approved, click **Send**. Carbon builds a PO document (PDF) and emails it to Ryerson's
contact. The order moves to **To Receive**. You can also download the PDF yourself.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">
  **Sending is not receiving.** Sending tells the supplier what you want — it does **not** add
  anything to inventory. Material lands on your shelf only when you *receive* it.
</Callout>

## Receive the goods

This is the step that actually changes your stock. When the truck arrives, create a
**receipt** against the PO. Receiving:

1. **Adds the material to inventory** at the deliver-to location.
2. **Records how much arrived** per line, so Carbon knows what's still outstanding.
3. **Records the lot** for batch-tracked items, for full
   [traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality).

A36 Steel Plate is batch-tracked, so the receipt asks for a lot number — that's how Acme can
later trace a hook back to the exact heat of steel it came from.

<Steps>

<Step>
### Start a receipt from the PO

Open PO000057 and click **Receive** (or start from **Inventory → Receipts** and pick the PO).
Carbon pre-loads the open lines with the outstanding quantity — here the full 54,001 LB.
</Step>

<Step>
### Confirm what arrived

Enter the **received quantity** per line. If they shipped everything, accept the suggested
amount; if part, enter the smaller number and Carbon keeps the rest open. Pick the **shelf /
bin** — Acme's A36 plate goes to **R02-B01-01** at Headquarters.
</Step>

<Step>
### Enter the batch (lot) number

Type the supplier's **lot number** from the mill certificate. This lot is now linked to the
material, so Carbon records which lot the job consumes.

*Receipt · A36 Steel Plate against PO000057. Receiving 54,001 LB into bin R02-B01-01, with the supplier's batch recorded.*

<Step>
### Post the receipt

Click **Post**. Carbon then:

- Increases **on-hand stock** at Headquarters by the received quantity.
- Stamps the PO line as received and moves the order toward **To Invoice**.
- Posts the **inventory value** to your books at the purchase cost.

The steel is now real, on the shelf, and ready for job `J000097`.

</Step>

</Steps>

## What happens next

<Mermaid
  chart={`flowchart LR
  N[Job needs material] --> PO[Purchase Order PO000057]
  PO --> AP[Approve and send to Ryerson]
  AP --> R[Receive 54,001 LB]
  R --> INV[A36 plate in inventory]
  INV --> J[Job J000097 consumes it]
  R --> BILL[Supplier invoice to pay]`}
/>

- The job **consumes** the plate, carrying the lot number into the finished hooks.
- The supplier sends a **bill**, which you match to the PO and receipt as an
  [accounts-payable invoice](/manual/accounting/overview). Matching PO ↔ receipt ↔ bill is how
  you catch an overcharge before you pay.
