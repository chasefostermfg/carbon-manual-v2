You took the order. Now you have to deliver it. This page follows the **back half** of a sale:
how a confirmed order becomes a finished, shipped, and invoiced job.

We use one real example all the way through — the 500 **Counterweight Hooks** (part
**CWH-A36-15**) Acme made for **Radiant Aerospace** on sales order **SO000069**.

## Where this lives in Carbon

This flow lives mostly in the **Sales** module, with one stop in **Inventory** (the shipment).
Open **Sales → Orders**, then click your order to see the buttons that drive everything below.

**Invoices**. You do not leave the order to ship or bill. You click these buttons, and Carbon
fills in the rest from the order.

## Step 1 — A Make-to-Order line creates a job

Each order line is set to **Make to Order** or **Pull from Inventory**.

- **Make to Order** — the part gets built. Confirming the order creates a **job**.
- **Pull from Inventory** — the part is already on a shelf, so you skip straight to shipping.

Line 1 on SO000069 is Make to Order. Confirming the order created job **J000097** for 500
pieces, due **2026-06-26**. The job links back to its order, customer, and quote — so the shop
floor knows *why* it's building these hooks.

## Step 2 — Watch fulfillment on the order

The sales order is your dashboard for "how close are we?" Every line keeps three running tallies,
updated automatically:

- **Ordered** — how many the customer asked for.
- **Shipped** — how many have left the dock.
- **Invoiced** — how many you've billed.

When **shipped** catches up to **ordered**, the line is delivered. When **invoiced** catches up
too, the line is done.

## Step 3 — Create the shipment

A **shipment** is the record of goods leaving your dock. It's also the event that bumps the
**shipped** tally. Start it from the order's **Shipments** button.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Click Shipments → New.** Carbon pre-fills the customer (Radiant Aerospace), the ship-to
address, and the open order lines.
</Step>

<Step>
**Pick the finished goods.** Enter how many of each line you're sending. All 500 hooks came in
from job J000097, so enter **500 EA**. Because A36 steel is batch-tracked, Carbon records which
batch ships — that's the [traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality) link.
</Step>

<Step>
**Choose a shipping method.** Pick the carrier and service you quoted. For a heavy steel order,
that's usually a freight method like **LTL (Less Than Truckload)**, not a parcel service.
</Step>

<Step>
**Post the shipment.** Posting removes the hooks from inventory and adds **500** to the line's
**shipped** count. The shipment is saved and linked to the order.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  You can ship a line in **more than one go**. Finish only 300 of 500? Ship 300 now — Carbon
  records a partial shipment and keeps the line open for the rest.
</Callout>

## Step 5 — Invoice the order

With the goods shipped, you can bill. A **sales invoice** is your request for payment. Carbon
builds it from what actually shipped, so it can't drift from reality. Start it from the order's
**Invoices** button.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Click Invoices → New.** Carbon drafts invoice **AR000025**, pulling line 1 in at the shipped
quantity (500) and the order price ($73.14).
</Step>

<Step>
**Check the totals.** Goods are 500 × $73.14 = **$36,570**. The **$250** shipping cost carries
over, making the total **$36,820**. Payment terms (**Net 30**) come from the customer record.
</Step>

<Step>
**Post and send it.** Posting books the revenue and marks the line fully invoiced. Send it to
Radiant; when they pay, mark it **Paid** — exactly how AR000025 ended, paid the same day.
</Step>

</Steps>
