A **customer** is anyone you sell to. Set one up once, and Carbon reuses their people, addresses,
and payment terms on every quote, order, and invoice — no re-typing.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Customers live in the **Sales** module. Open **Sales** from the home screen, then click
**Customers** in the sub-nav across the top.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-dashboard.png"
  alt="The Sales module dashboard with the Customers sub-nav at the top"
  caption="Sales module. Use the sub-nav (Customers, Quotes, Orders) to jump between Sales screens."
/>

The **Customers** list shows one row per company. We'll build out **Radiant Aerospace** — Acme's
most active customer, behind the Counterweight Hook order **SO000069**.

## Two things to know first

Before you add customers, two fields are worth a quick look.

**Status** — shows where a customer is in your relationship:

- <StatusBadge tone="blue">Lead</StatusBadge> — a prospect you're quoting, no order yet.
- <StatusBadge tone="green">Active</StatusBadge> — a live customer you can quote, order, and invoice.
- <StatusBadge tone="purple">On Hold</StatusBadge> — paused, often for a payment issue.
- <StatusBadge tone="neutral">Inactive</StatusBadge> — no longer trading, kept for history.
- <StatusBadge tone="red">Cancelled</StatusBadge> — relationship closed.

**Type** — a label for grouping customers, handy for filtering and reports. Acme groups by
industry. Radiant Aerospace is **Aerospace & Defense**.

<Callout type="tip">
  Both lists are editable under **Settings**. Set up your statuses and types once so new customers
  land in the right bucket. See [Set up your company](/manual/getting-started/setup-company).
</Callout>

## Create a customer

Only a **name** is required. Filling in the rest now saves re-keying later.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the form

On the **Customers** list, click **New Customer** (top right).

</Step>

<Step>

### Enter the basics

Give the customer a **name**, then set the optional **type**, **status**, and **currency**.

<AppShot title="New Customer" url="app.carbon.ms/x/sales/customers/new" caption="Creating Radiant Aerospace.">
  <FieldGrid cols={2}>
    <Field label="Name" value="Radiant Aerospace" />
    <Field label="Customer type" value="Aerospace & Defense" />
    <Field label="Status" value="Active" />
    <Field label="Currency" value="USD" />
  </FieldGrid>
</AppShot>

</Step>

<Step>

### Save

Click **Save**. Carbon assigns a customer ID automatically (Radiant's internal ID is
`cust_LxoH7AbowNVjTwGSoqkW1Q`). The customer now appears on the list, and you can add the rest
anytime.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Add contacts

A **contact** is a person at the customer — a buyer, an engineer, an AP clerk. You pick a contact
when you quote or order, and Carbon emails them the PDF.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the Contacts tab

On the customer record, click the **Contacts** tab, then **New Contact**.

</Step>

<Step>

### Enter their details

Fill in name, **email**, phone, and (optionally) job title. Email matters most — it's what Carbon
uses to send quotes and orders.

</Step>

<Step>

### Save and repeat

Save, then add anyone else you deal with — for example a separate AP contact for invoices.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Contacts follow the paperwork. The contact you pick on an order flows straight onto its invoice —
  on Radiant's order **SO000069**, the same contact appeared on invoice **AR000025**.
</Callout>

## Add ship-to and bill-to locations

A **location** is an address. Carbon uses two roles:

- **Ship-to** — where goods are delivered. Drives [shipments](/manual/inventory/shipping-receiving).
- **Bill-to** — where invoices go. Drives [accounting](/manual/accounting/overview).

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the Locations tab

On the customer record, click the **Locations** tab, then **New Location**.

</Step>

<Step>

### Enter the address and role

Type the address, then tag it **Ship-to**, **Bill-to**, or both. Mark one as the **default** so
Carbon pre-fills it on new orders. Radiant uses one HQ address for both roles — the most common
setup.

</Step>

<Step>

### Save

Save each address. Carbon proposes the defaults on every new order, and you can switch in one click.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Set payment terms

**Payment terms** say *when* a customer should pay — for example **Net 30** means "due 30 days
after the invoice date." Set them on the customer and every invoice inherits the right due date.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the Payment tab

On the customer record, click the **Payment** tab.

</Step>

<Step>

### Pick the terms

Choose the default **payment terms** and **currency**. Radiant Aerospace is **Net 30** in **USD**.

<AppShot title="Payment · Radiant Aerospace" url="app.carbon.ms/x/sales/customers/…/payment" caption="Radiant's default billing terms.">
  <FieldGrid cols={3}>
    <Field label="Payment terms" value="Net 30" />
    <Field label="Currency" value="USD" />
    <Field label="Tax" value="None (0%)" />
  </FieldGrid>
</AppShot>

</Step>

<Step>

### Save

Save. From now on, invoices for this customer default to **Net 30** — exactly what shows on
Radiant's real invoice **AR000025**.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Terms are defaults, not handcuffs. You can override them on a single order or invoice anytime.
</Callout>

## Customer-specific part numbers

Your customer often calls your part by *their* number. Acme's part is `CWH-A36-15`, but Radiant
orders it as **730562**. Record that mapping once on the customer, and Carbon recognizes their PO
references and prints their number on your paperwork.
