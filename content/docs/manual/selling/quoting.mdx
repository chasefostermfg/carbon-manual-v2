---
title: "Quoting & estimating"
description: "Build a quote, price it, send it and turn it into an order."
---

A **quote** answers a customer's "what would this cost?" You add a line for the part and a
quantity, Carbon rolls up the cost behind it, and you set a price. When the customer says yes,
one click turns the quote into a **sales order** — nothing gets re-typed.

We'll follow Acme's real quote **Q000061**: 500 Counterweight Hooks for **Radiant Aerospace**
at **$73.14 each**.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Quotes live in the **Sales** module. Open **Sales → Quotes** to see every quote. Click a row
to open one, or click the green **Add Quote** button (top-right) to start a new one.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-quotes.png"
  alt="The Quotes list in the Sales module, with Q000061 for Radiant Aerospace marked Ordered"
  caption="Sales → Quotes. Click a row to open a quote, or the green Add Quote button to start one."
/>

<Callout type="info">
  A quote is a **price offer, not a commitment**. Nothing gets made, bought, or billed until
  you convert it to a [sales order](/manual/selling/sales-orders). So it's the safe place to
  try quantities and adjust your margin.
</Callout>

## Build the quote

<Steps>

<Step>
**Start it.** Click the green **Add Quote** button, then pick **Radiant Aerospace** as the
customer. Carbon pulls in their address, contact, currency (USD) and payment terms
(**Net 30**) — so you don't re-key any of it.
</Step>

<Step>
**Add a line.** Click **Add Line**, set the type to **Part**, and choose **Counterweight
Hook** (`CWH-A36-15`). Carbon fills in the description and unit (**EA**, Each) from the item.
</Step>

<Step>
**Enter the quantity.** Type **500**. Want to show a price ladder? Add more quantities to the
same line (say 100, 500, 1,000) and Carbon prices each one.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
  Quotes start in **Draft** — yours to edit freely before the customer sees them. Carbon also
  assigns the number (`Q000061`) and a revision automatically.
</Callout>

## Price the line

For a **Make to Order** part, Carbon already knows the recipe — what it's made of and how it's
made. So it rolls up a real cost instead of asking you to guess.

The Counterweight Hook is short: **40 lb of 1-inch A36 steel plate**, then three operations.

You set the **price**; Carbon shows the **margin** — the gap between what the work costs and
what you charge. Type a price and the margin updates live; type a target margin and Carbon
back-solves the price. Acme set **$73.14 each**:

<StatGrid cols={3}>

  <Stat label="Quantity" value="500 EA" hint="this price break" />

</StatGrid>

<Callout type="warn">
  The cost rollup is only as good as the part's [method](/manual/items/methods). No bill of
  materials or routing yet? Carbon can't estimate the cost — build the method first, or quote
  a known price and refine it later.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip">
  Selling the same *kind* of part in many shapes (cut plate, machined bar)? Use a
  **configurator** — one item that asks a few questions, then builds the method and cost for
  you. Acme has the **Flame Cut Part Configurator** (`FLAME CONFIG`). See
  [Methods: BOM & routing](/manual/items/methods).
</Callout>

## Send it

<Steps>

<Step>
**Check the total.** On Q000061 that's one line: 500 EA × $73.14 = **$36,570.00**.
</Step>

<Step>
**Click Send.** Carbon builds the PDF, emails it to Radiant's contact, and moves the status
from **Draft** to **Sent** — the ball is now in the customer's court.
</Step>

</Steps>

## Convert to an order

When Radiant says yes, don't rebuild anything. On the quote, click **Convert to Order**.
Carbon copies the customer, lines, quantities and prices onto a new
[sales order](/manual/selling/sales-orders) — here, **SO000069** — and the two stay linked.

<AppShot
  src="/screenshots/manual/sales-order-detail.png"
  alt="Sales Order SO000069 for Radiant Aerospace, converted from quote Q000061"
  caption="The converted order SO000069. Same part, same price — now a commitment, still linked to Q000061."
/>

<Callout type="info">
  The order total ($36,820.00) is higher than the quote line total ($36,570.00) because $250
  of shipping was added on the order. Quotes price the *work*; the order is where freight and
  taxes get finalized.
</Callout>

Converting marks the quote **Ordered** (so you can always see what you offered) instead of
deleting it. A quote the customer passes on can be marked **Lost** instead — which keeps your
win/loss history honest.
