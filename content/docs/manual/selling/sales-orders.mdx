A **sales order** is your customer's commitment to buy: who's ordering, what, how many,
when it's due, and at what price. Enter it once and Carbon carries it everywhere — the
[job](/manual/manufacturing/jobs) that makes the part, the [shipment](/manual/selling/sales-to-shipment)
that sends it, and the invoice that bills for it.

This page follows Acme's real order **SO000069** — 500 Counterweight Hooks for Radiant
Aerospace, **$36,820**, *Make to Order*.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Sales orders live in the **Sales** module. Open **Sales**, then click **Orders** in the
left sub-nav to see every order and its status.

The list shows each order's number, customer, total, and **status** at a glance. Click a
row to open the order.

## Two parts of an order

Every sales order has two parts:

- A **header** — facts for the whole order: the customer, the order date, the
  [payment terms](/manual/overview/glossary), the ship-from location, and the customer's own
  PO number.
- One or more **lines** — each line is one thing the customer is buying: an item, a quantity,
  a unit price, a promised date, and a **fulfillment method**.

Here's SO000069 open. The line **CWH-A36-15** is 500 EA at **$73.14**. The tabs along the
top — **Shipments** and **Invoices** — show what went out and what was billed.

## Create a sales order

There are two ways an order is born. **Converting a quote** is by far the most common — it's
how SO000069 was created.

<Tabs items={["Convert a quote", "Start from scratch"]}>
<Tab value="Convert a quote">

When a customer accepts a [quote](/manual/selling/quoting), you don't re-type anything.
Carbon copies the lines, quantities, prices, and the linked [method](/manual/items/methods),
and keeps the two records linked.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Open the accepted quote.** Go to **Sales → Quotes** and open it — for Acme that's
`Q000061`. Confirm the line, quantity, and price.
</Step>

<Step>
**Convert to Order.** Click **Convert to Order** in the top-right. Carbon creates `SO000069`
and copies every line, including the *Make to Order* method on the hook line.
</Step>

<Step>
**Review the header.** Confirm the **customer PO reference** (`55557777`) and the
**ship-from location** (Headquarters). Set the **order date** if it isn't today.
</Step>

</Steps>

</Tab>
<Tab value="Start from scratch">

Not every order comes from a quote — a repeat customer might just email a PO.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Add a new order.** In **Sales → Orders**, click the green **Add** button, top-right. Pick
the [customer](/manual/selling/customers) (Radiant Aerospace). Carbon fills in their default
terms, currency, and ship-to address.
</Step>

<Step>
**Fill in the header.** Set the **order date**, the **ship-from location**, and the
customer's **PO reference**. Save to get an order number.
</Step>

<Step>
**Add the lines.** Add one line per item — covered next.
</Step>

</Steps>

</Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout type="tip">
  The **customer PO reference** (`55557777`) is the number *your customer* uses for the order.
  Capturing it prints it on the packing slip and invoice, so their accounts-payable team can
  match your bill without a phone call.
</Callout>

## Pick a fulfillment method

The **fulfillment method** is the most important choice on a line. You set it **per line**, so
one order can ship some items from stock and make others to order.

<DataTable
  columns={[
    { key: "method", label: "Method" },
    { key: "when", label: "Use it when…" },
    { key: "does", label: "What Carbon does" },
  ]}
  rows={[
    { method: <Tag>Make to Order</Tag>, when: "The item is built for this order.", does: "Spawns a job to make it." },
    { method: <Tag>Pull from Stock</Tag>, when: "You keep the finished item on a shelf.", does: "Reserves on-hand inventory. No job." },
    { method: <Tag>Drop Ship</Tag>, when: "A supplier ships straight to your customer.", does: "Links a PO that delivers to the customer." },
  ]}
/>

SO000069's line is **Make to Order** — Acme doesn't stock 500 finished hooks, so each order is
built fresh. That one setting spawns **Job J000097** when the order is confirmed.

<Callout type="info">
  A line's default method comes from the item's
  [replenishment setting](/manual/items/replenishment): a *Make* part defaults to *Make to
  Order*, a *Buy* part to *Pull from Stock*. You can override it on any line.
</Callout>

## Track shipments and invoices

The **Shipments** and **Invoices** buttons on the order are where fulfillment shows up. You
don't fill them in by hand — they populate as goods leave and bills post.

- **Shipments** — every time goods leave, a shipment record appears with the quantity and date.
  SO000069 has one shipment for all 500 hooks, sent **2026-06-11**.
- **Invoices** — when you bill, the invoice pulls its lines from what shipped. SO000069 was
  billed on `AR000025` for **$36,820** (goods plus $250 shipping) and marked **Paid** the same day.

<Callout type="info">
  Radiant Aerospace keeps coming back: the same hook runs on **SO000070** (1,500 EA, in
  progress) and **SO000071** (3,000 EA, queued). Each is its own order with its own job, but all
  point back to the same item and method.
</Callout>

## Common gotchas

<Accordions type="single">
<Accordion title="I confirmed the order but no job appeared.">
Check the line's **fulfillment method**. A job is only created for **Make to Order** lines. If
the line is *Pull from Stock*, Carbon reserves inventory instead — no job.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="The price on the line isn't what I quoted.">
Lines copied from a quote carry the quoted price. A line you add by hand uses the item's default
sell price, which may differ. When in doubt, convert from the quote rather than re-keying.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Can one order ship in more than one batch?">
Yes. A line can ship partially — send what's ready now, the rest later. The line stays *To Ship*
until the full quantity has gone out. The order reaches *Completed* only when every line is fully
shipped and invoiced.
</Accordion>
</Accordions>
