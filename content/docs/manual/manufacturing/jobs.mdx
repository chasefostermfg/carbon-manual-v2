A **job** tells the shop floor to *make* a set quantity of a part by a set date.

We'll follow one real job: `J000097` — **500 Counterweight Hooks** for Radiant Aerospace.

## Where this lives in Carbon

Jobs live in the **Production** module. Open it from the home screen, and the dashboard shows
your live shop at a glance — **Active Jobs**, work center use, and more. Click **View Active
Jobs** to see the full list.

## What a job carries

One job answers five questions:

- **What** are we making? The part — here, `CWH-A36-15`, the Counterweight Hook.
- **How many?** The production quantity — **500 EA**.
- **By when?** The due date — **2026-06-26**.
- **How is it made?** The [method](/manual/items/methods) — its materials and steps.
- **Where are we?** The status, and how many good pieces are done.

The big idea: **a job carries its own copy of the method.** When you create it, Carbon
*snapshots* the part's bill of materials and routing onto the job. Edit the part's master
method later — this job keeps the recipe it was born with.

That's what keeps a finished job's cost and
[traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality) trustworthy months later.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Method = BOM + Routing.** The **bill of materials (BOM)** is *what the part is made of*;
  the **routing** is *the ordered steps to make it*. A job copies both. See
  [Methods: BOM &amp; routing](/manual/items/methods).
</Callout>

## How a job gets created

There are two ways.

**1. Automatically, from a Make-to-Order sales order line.** Carbon offers to spin up a job,
pre-filled with the part, quantity, customer and due date — and keeps it linked to the line.
That's how `J000097` was born, straight from
[sales order SO000069](/manual/selling/sales-orders).

<Callout type="info">
  The same link happens for Radiant's other orders: `SO000070` made job `J000098` (1,500 EA)
  and `SO000071` made job `J000099` (3,000 EA). One part, three orders, three jobs.
</Callout>

**2. Manually, when a job stands on its own** — building to stock, a rework batch, or a
one-off. Here's the click path:

<Steps>

<Step>
**Open the Jobs list.** In the **Production** module, click **View Active Jobs** (or open
**Jobs**). Click **New Job**.
</Step>

<Step>
**Pick the part.** Choose a *Make* [item](/manual/items/defining-an-item) like `CWH-A36-15`.
Carbon pulls in its description and unit of measure (EA).
</Step>

<Step>
**Set quantity, due date and priority.** Enter how many to make, when they're due, and how
urgent the job is.
</Step>

<Step>
**Save.** Carbon assigns the next job number and snapshots the method. The job opens as
**Planned**, ready for review.
</Step>

</Steps>

- **Production quantity** — how many good pieces to finish. Here, 500.
- **Due date** — when they must be done. The **deadline type** (**Hard Deadline** here) tells
  Carbon how firm that date is.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Location matters.** A job runs at one
  [location](/manual/inventory/locations-and-stock). `J000097` runs at **Headquarters**, so it
  uses Headquarters work centers (like Burn Table — PA2) and pulls material from Headquarters
  stock.
</Callout>

Click the **Method** tab to see the recipe Carbon copied onto the job. For the Counterweight
Hook it's short: one material, three operations.

Two things to notice:

- The BOM has one material line: **40 LB** of A36 steel plate *per piece*. Times 500, the job
  needs **20,000 LB** — Carbon does the math.
- The material is **Pull from Inventory**, so the job draws steel from stock. The
  [purchase order to Ryerson](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics) makes sure that stock is there.

<Callout type="info">
  Because the job carries its *own* copy of the method, you can tweak it for this run only —
  swap a material, add a step, change a time — without touching the part's master method or any
  other job.
</Callout>

### Operations: the steps, in order

The **Operations** tab lists the routing. Each operation runs at one **work center** and has a
time per piece. They run in sequence — Sandblasting can't start until Flame Cut is done.

Labor time is *per piece*. As operators report finished pieces in the

  The last operation, **Inspection**, is a quality gate: the parts aren't finished until they
  pass. See [Tracking quality](/manual/quality/tracking-quality).

## Releasing a job to the floor

A new job is **Planned** — a draft you can edit freely. Nothing reaches the floor until you
**release** it.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Review the job.** Confirm quantity, due date and priority on **Details**, then skim
**Method**, **Operations** and **Materials**.
</Step>

<Step>
**Check material.** Carbon flags shortages. For `J000097`, not all 20,000 LB of steel was on
hand, so Acme raised [PO000057](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics) to Ryerson. Buying and making
can run in parallel.
</Step>

<Step>
**Release the job.** Click **Release**. Carbon stamps the released date, moves the job into the
active queue, and makes its operations available in the
[shop-floor app](/manual/manufacturing/shop-floor).
</Step>

<Step>
**The floor takes over.** Operators clock onto each operation and report pieces. When the last
operation hits the production quantity, the job is **Completed**.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">

  *Planned*.
</Callout>

and billed on [invoice AR000025](/manual/accounting/overview). The job's labor and material
rolled up into the cost of those parts.
