This page is about the two moments when physical goods actually cross your dock: stuff
coming **in** from a supplier (**receiving**), and finished goods going **out** to a
customer (**shipping**). In Carbon these aren't loose warehouse chores — each one points
back to a document you already created. A [receipt](/manual/overview/glossary) settles a
**purchase order** line; a [shipment](/manual/overview/glossary) settles a **sales order**
line and feeds the invoice. Do them right and your on-hand counts, your costs, and your
customer's bill all stay in sync on their own.

<Callout type="info">
  **Where this fits.** Receiving is the back half of [purchasing](/manual/buying/purchasing-basics) —
  you raised a PO, now the goods arrive. Shipping is the back half of
  [selling](/manual/selling/sales-to-shipment) — you made the parts on a
  [job](/manual/manufacturing/jobs), now they leave. Both flows read and write the
  [stock](/manual/inventory/locations-and-stock) you keep in your locations and bins.
</Callout>

## Two flows, one idea

A **receipt** and a **shipment** are mirror images of each other. One adds to inventory,
the other subtracts from it. Both work the same way: you start from a source document,
confirm the real-world quantities, optionally record *which* physical material moved (its
lot or batch), and then **post** — the moment Carbon commits the change to your books.

<Mermaid
  chart={`flowchart LR
  PO[Purchase Order PO000057] --> R[Receipt]
  R --> INV[Inventory goes up]
  INV --> JOB[Job consumes material]
  JOB --> FG[Finished goods]
  FG --> SH[Shipment]
  SH --> SO[Sales Order SO000069 updated]
  SH --> AR[Invoice AR000025]`}
/>

We'll use Acme's hero order to walk both ends. The A36 steel for the Counterweight Hooks
arrives on **PO000057** from Ryerson — that's receiving. The 500 finished hooks leave for
Radiant Aerospace against **SO000069** — that's shipping.

<Callout type="tip">
  **"Post" is the verb that matters.** Up until you post, a receipt or shipment is a draft
  you can still edit or delete — nothing has changed in inventory. Posting is the
  irreversible commit: stock levels move, costs land, and downstream documents update. Get
  the numbers right *before* you post.
</Callout>

## Flow 1 — Receiving against a purchase order

When a truck backs up to the dock with material you ordered, you create a **receipt**
against the matching purchase order. Carbon already knows what you expected — the part,
the quantity, the price — so receiving is mostly confirming reality and noting any
differences.

For the hero thread, Acme ordered **54,001 LB** of A36 Steel Plate from
[Ryerson](/manual/buying/suppliers) on **PO000057**. Here's the open PO line waiting to be
received.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Open the PO and start a receipt

Go to **Purchasing → Purchase Orders**, open **PO000057**, and choose **Receive** (or start
from **Inventory → Receipts → New** and pick the PO). Carbon copies the open lines onto a
fresh receipt so you don't re-type anything. The receipt starts as a **Draft** — safe to
edit.

<Callout type="warn">
  A purchase order has to be **issued** to the supplier before you can receive against it.
  PO000057 is currently *Needs Approval* — in a real flow it would be approved and sent
  first. You can't receive goods against a PO that's still a draft.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>

### Confirm the quantities that actually arrived

The receipt pre-fills each line with the quantity still outstanding. Edit the **received
quantity** to match what the packing slip and your scale say. Three common cases:

- **Exact** — the truck brought all 54,001 LB. Leave the line as-is.
- **Short** — only part of the order came. Receive what arrived; the PO line stays open for
  the rest (a **partial receipt**), and you can receive the balance on a later delivery.
- **Over** — more came than you ordered. Carbon flags the overage so you can accept it or
  send it back, per your purchasing rules.

</Step>

<Step>

### Record the lot or batch for tracked items

A36 Steel Plate is **batch-tracked** (`requiresBatchTracking: true`). That means Carbon
won't just add "20,000 lb of steel" to a faceless pile — it wants to know *which heat* of
steel this is, so that months later you can answer "exactly which material went into
Radiant's hooks?" Type the supplier's heat/lot number into the **batch** field on the line.

<Callout type="tip">
  **Serial vs batch.** Carbon's `itemTrackingType` decides how granular this gets.
  *Inventory* items (like the finished hook) just track a count. *Batch* items track by
  lot — one number for the whole delivery. *Serial* items track every single unit
  individually. You only see a batch or serial field when the item asks for one, so for
  most parts this step simply doesn't appear. More in
  [Locations & stock](/manual/inventory/locations-and-stock).
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>

### Tell Carbon where it goes

Each received line needs a home: a **location** and a **shelf** (bin). Acme's steel lives
at the **Headquarters** location in bin **R02-B01-01** — its default — so the receipt
suggests that automatically. Change it if you're stocking it somewhere else.

</Step>

<Step>

### Post the receipt

When the numbers and batch are right, choose **Post**. At that instant:

- On-hand stock of A36 Steel Plate **goes up** by the received quantity in bin R02-B01-01.
- The PO line's `quantityReceived` updates, moving the PO toward *Received* / *To Invoice*.
- Carbon posts the value to your **Inventory** account and to **GR/IR Clearing** (the
  holding account between goods-received and invoice-received) — see
  [Accounting overview](/manual/accounting/overview).

The material is now real stock the [job](/manual/manufacturing/jobs) can pull from. The
Counterweight Hook job needs 40 LB per piece × 500 = **20,000 LB**, and it now has steel to
consume.

</Step>

</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
  **The same dock handles services and outside work.** If a PO line is for an *Outside
  Operation* (you sent parts out for, say, anodizing), receiving it back is the same motion —
  it tells Carbon the subcontracted step is done and the parts are home.
</Callout>

## Flow 2 — Shipping against a sales order

Once the parts are made and inspected, you send them. A **shipment** records what physically
left the building, against the sales order (and the job behind it). The hero job
**J000097** finished all 500 Counterweight Hooks and received them into inventory
(`quantityReceivedToInventory: 500`); now those 500 ship to Radiant Aerospace on
**SO000069**.

<Steps>

<Step>

### Start a shipment from the order

Open **SO000069** under **Sales → Sales Orders** and choose **Ship** (or go to **Inventory →
Shipments → New** and select the order). Carbon creates a shipment and brings the order
lines across. As with receipts, it begins as a **Draft** you can adjust before posting.

</Step>

<Step>

### Pick the finished goods

**Picking** is choosing which stock fills the shipment. For the hooks, Carbon pulls from the
job's finished-goods bin (the job stored its output in storage unit **8-1-H**). Confirm the
quantity to ship on each line — here, all **500 EA**.

If a part is batch- or serial-tracked, you pick the specific lots/serials here, and Carbon
records them on the shipment. That closes the [traceability](/manual/quality/tracking-quality)
loop: you'll be able to trace this shipment back to the exact heat of A36 steel you received
in Flow 1.

<Callout type="warn">
  **You can only ship what you have.** Carbon won't let you ship more than is on hand in the
  picking location. If the job isn't finished or the finished goods haven't been received
  into inventory yet, the quantity you can pick is capped. Short shipments are fine — the SO
  line stays open for the balance.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>

### Choose a shipping method

A **shipping method** is the carrier and service the goods leave on — it prints on paperwork
and tells everyone how the parts are travelling. Acme has **ten** methods configured, from
carrier overnight to a customer picking up themselves. Pick the one that matches the order.

  A pallet of 500 steel hooks is heavy freight, not a parcel — so **LTL (Less Than
  Truckload)** or **FTL (Full Truckload)** is the right call here, not UPS Ground. The
  method you choose is also where a **shipping cost** rides along: SO000069 carries **$250**
  of freight, which shows up on the invoice but not in the goods subtotal.
</Callout>

</Step>

<Step>

### Add tracking details (optional)

If the carrier gives you a tracking number, add it to the shipment along with package
counts and weights. It's optional, but it travels with the record so anyone looking at the
order later can answer "where is it?" without leaving Carbon.

</Step>

<Step>

### Post the shipment

Choose **Post** to commit. At that instant a cascade fires:

- The 500 hooks **leave inventory** from the picking bin.
- The SO line's `quantitySent` updates to **500 of 500**, and because the whole line shipped
  it's flagged `sentComplete` — the order moves toward *Completed*.
- The job's `quantityShipped` updates to 500.
- A **shipment record** is created (`sh_7injMjdu1xyZqTVFFrjauv`) — the link the
  [invoice](/manual/accounting/overview) reads from next.

## What posting the shipment sets in motion

The whole point of tying shipments to the sales order is what happens *after* you post. You
recorded one fact — "500 hooks left the dock" — and Carbon updates everything that depends
on it.

<StatGrid cols={3}>
  <Stat label="Shipped" value="500 / 500" hint="SO000069 line, sentComplete" />
  <Stat label="On-hand" value="−500 EA" hint="left the finished-goods bin" />
  <Stat label="Invoice" value="AR000025" hint="pulls lines from this shipment" />
</StatGrid>

Because the shipment is posted, Acme can now raise the **sales invoice**. Carbon builds
**AR000025** straight from what shipped — 500 EA at $73.14, plus the $250 freight — so the
bill matches the truck, with no re-keying. The invoice references this exact shipment
(`shipmentId: sh_7injMjdu1xyZqTVFFrjauv`), and once Radiant pays it lands as **Paid**. That
is the receiving-to-shipping arc closing into cash.

  **One number, entered once.** You never told Carbon "the order is complete" or "bill the
  customer." You said "500 hooks shipped," and the sales order line, the job, the shipment
  record, and the invoice all updated themselves. That's the system-of-record payoff.
</Callout>

## Common questions

<Accordions type="single">

<Accordion title="Can I receive or ship part of an order?">
  Yes. Receipts and shipments are per-line and per-quantity. Receive 30,000 of 54,001 LB
  today and the PO line stays open for the rest; ship 200 of 500 hooks and the SO line keeps
  the remaining 300 open until a later shipment. Carbon only marks a line "complete" once the
  full quantity has moved.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if I posted a receipt or shipment with the wrong quantity?">
  Posting is a committed inventory transaction, so you don't simply edit it after the fact.
  You reverse the effect with a correcting movement — for example a negative inventory
  adjustment, a return, or a credit — which keeps a clean audit trail of what really
  happened rather than silently overwriting history. Fix it before you post whenever you can.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I have to record a batch or serial number?">
  Only when the item asks for one. The field appears for items set to track by batch or
  serial — A36 Steel Plate, for example, has `requiresBatchTracking: true`. For plain
  *Inventory* items like the finished Counterweight Hook, Carbon just tracks a quantity and
  the batch/serial step never shows up.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What's a 'drop shipment'?">
  A drop shipment is when a supplier ships goods straight to your customer, so they never pass
  through your dock. SO000069 has `dropShipment: false` — Acme made and shipped the hooks
  itself. When a line *is* drop-shipped, Carbon links the supplier delivery to the customer
  order directly, and you don't pick or post a shipment yourself.
</Accordion>

</Accordions>
