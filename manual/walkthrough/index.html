Carbon in 15 minutes

Follow one real order from quote to cash — and see how Carbon's modules connect.

The fastest way to "get" Carbon is to follow one order through it. We'll track a single real part — a Counterweight Hook that Acme makes for Radiant Aerospace — from the first price request to a paid invoice. You'll touch most of the Modules along the way.

The part: the Counterweight Hook (part number CWH-A36-15) is a steel hook cut from 1-inch plate. Radiant orders 500 at a time. You don't need to do anything here — just read.

Here's the whole journey. Each step happens in a different Module, and Carbon links them all together automatically:

Loading diagram...

Step 1 — Quote the customer · Sales module

Radiant asks "what do 500 hooks cost?" You answer with a quote. Open the Sales module, go to Quotes, and you'll see every quote — here's Q000061 for Radiant, already marked Ordered (they said yes).

You add a line for the part and quantity; Carbon adds up the material and shop time so you can set a price. When the customer says yes, one click turns the quote into a sales order.

Step 2 — Take the order · Sales module

The sales order is the customer's commitment. In Sales → Orders you can see SO000069 for Radiant Aerospace — Completed, $36,820.

Open the order and you see the line: CWH-A36-15, 500 each at $73.14. Notice it's set to Make to Order — so Carbon knows it has to be built, and it already created a job for it.

Step 3 — How it's made · Items module

Every part you make has a Method — a recipe with two halves: the Bill of Material (what it's made of) and the routing (the steps to make it). Open the part in the Items module to see it.

Each hook needs 40 lb of steel, so 500 hooks need 20,000 lb. Carbon already knows that — watch what it does next.

Step 4 — Make it · Production module

Because the order is Make to Order, Carbon creates a job (J000097) for 500 pieces. The Production module is mission control for the shop — here you can see active jobs and how busy each work center is.

On the shop floor, operators open the Shop Floor app, tap onto each step, and record how many good pieces they finished. That's how the job gets marked done — and how its real cost adds up.

Step 5 — Buy the steel · Purchasing module

Acme is short on 1-inch plate, so Carbon flags it and you raise a purchase order to your steel supplier, Ryerson (PO000057). The Purchasing module tracks all of this.

Because A36 plate is batch-tracked, Carbon records exactly which steel went into this batch of hooks — the start of full traceability.

Step 6 — Check it and ship it · Quality + Inventory

The job's last step is Inspection — a quality gate. Once the parts pass, you create a shipment and send the 500 hooks. Carbon marks the order line Shipped.

Step 7 — Get paid · Accounting module

Finally you bill the order. The sales invoice (AR000025) pulls its lines from what shipped — no re-typing — and once Radiant pays, it's marked Paid.
