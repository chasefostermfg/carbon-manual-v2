Setting up your company

Company profile, locations, units of measure and number sequences.

Tell Carbon a few facts about your business — who you are, where you keep stock, how you measure things, how documents are numbered, and when you get paid. Set these up once, up front. We'll use the real settings behind Acme Inc., the example company in this manual.

Where this lives in Carbon

Everything here lives in the Settings module. Open it from the gear icon at the bottom-left of the navigation rail.

Settings is split into sections: Company, Locations, Units of Measure, Sequences, Payment Terms, and more. You'll set up five of them below.

You need an administrator account to change these settings. You can edit most of them later — except your base currency. Pick that one carefully.

1. Company profile

Your company profile is your business's ID card. Carbon stamps it onto every document you send — quotes, order confirmations, purchase orders and invoices.

Open Settings → Company.

Enter your name and address. Acme is "Acme Inc." at 2400 Anvil Way, Springfield, OH.

Upload your logo (PNG or SVG). It shows on PDFs and in the top-left of the app.

Set your base currency. Acme uses USD. This is the currency your books are kept in.

Click Save.

Don't change your base currency after you start transacting — it isn't a supported flow. If you picked wrong, start a fresh company instead.

2. Locations

A location is a physical site where you make things, hold stock, or ship from. Carbon tracks inventory and work centers per location. Acme runs two.

  • Headquarters is Acme's home base. It runs the Counterweight Hook thread you'll follow in this manual (SO000069, J000097, PO000057).
  • Texas is a second site with its own burn table and laser table.

Shelves and bins live inside a location

Inside a location you can say exactly where stock sits, using storage units (racks, rows, levels, bins). Acme's A36 steel plate for the Hook job lives in bin R02-B01-01. You don't need these on day one — one catch-all bin per location is enough to start.

Add a location for every site that holds inventory or ships product. One building? One location is fine. Just don't lump two separate warehouses together, or your on-hand counts won't match reality. More in Locations & stock.

3. Units of measure

A unit of measure (UoM) is how you count a thing — each, pounds, gallons, feet. Every item has one. The Counterweight Hook is counted in EA (Each); the steel plate it's cut from is measured in LB (Pound). Acme runs 16 in total.

To add your own, open Settings → Units of Measure and give it a short code (the abbreviation that shows on documents) and a name.

Set an item's unit before you transact with it — it feeds pricing, inventory and purchasing. Buying in one unit and stocking in another (buy steel by the pound, receive it as plates) uses separate purchase and inventory units, not a change to the base unit.

4. Number sequences

Every document gets a short, readable ID — Q000061, SO000069, J000097, PO000057, AR000025. You don't type these; Carbon builds each one from a number sequence: a prefix plus a running counter.

Each sequence has three parts you can tune:

  • Prefix — the letters in front. AR is accounts receivable (money owed to you), AP is accounts payable (money you owe).
  • Padding — how many digits, zero-filled. Acme pads to six, so SO000069, not SO69.
  • Next number — the counter, which ticks up by one each time a document is created.

Acme's hero order flows through these sequences: quote Q000061 becomes sales order SO000069, spawns job J000097, triggers purchase order PO000057, and finishes with invoice AR000025.

Set prefixes and padding before you create your first document. The counter only goes up — don't reset it backwards, or you risk duplicate IDs.

5. Payment terms

Payment terms say when an invoice is due — and sometimes offer a discount for paying early. Attach a default to each customer and supplier. Carbon copies it onto their orders and invoices and works out the due date. Acme keeps seven.

Net 30 is Acme's default and the term on the hero thread — on Radiant Aerospace's order SO000069, the Ryerson purchase order PO000057, and the invoice AR000025.

Set one term as the company default, optionally override it on a specific customer or supplier, and Carbon fills it in automatically on every new order and invoice. You can still change it on one document when a deal calls for it.
