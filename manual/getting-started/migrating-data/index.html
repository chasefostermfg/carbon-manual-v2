Migrating your data

Bring your items, customers and suppliers into Carbon.

Moving from spreadsheets or another system? You don't retype everything by hand. You import it with Carbon's built-in CSV importers. The one rule: import the things other records depend on first.

We'll use Acme Inc.'s real data as the example throughout.

Where this lives in Carbon

Each importer lives in the module that owns the data — Items, Sales, Purchasing, Inventory. You open that module, click Import, and upload a CSV. Imported parts land in the Items module, on the Parts list.

Import in this order

Carbon records are linked. A part needs a unit of measure. Opening stock needs the part and a location. A sales order needs a customer and an item. So you load the bottom layer first and build up.

You don't have to migrate history. Most shops bring in master data (items, customers, suppliers) plus a snapshot of opening inventory and any open orders — and leave closed jobs and paid invoices in the old system. A clean start beats a perfect copy.

1 — Units & locations

A unit of measure (UoM) is how you count an item: EA for each, LB for pounds. A location is a site that holds stock. Carbon ships with the common UoMs, so usually you're just confirming they exist.

Acme keeps it small — EA for parts, LB for raw materials — and runs two sites, Headquarters and Texas.

Pick your UoM and location codes now and never rename them. Every imported row points at these codes by exact text — change EA to ea later and you orphan thousands of references.

2 — Items

Items are the heart of Carbon — a part you make, a material you buy, a tool. This is the biggest import. Each CSV row becomes one item.

Two decisions matter on every row: its itemType (part, material, tool?) and its replenishmentSystem — do you Make it or Buy it?

Import opening inventory once, ideally on go-live day. If you import balances and keep transacting in the old system, the two drift apart fast. Freeze, count, import, run forward.

5 — BOMs & methods

A method is how a made item is built: its bill of materials (what it's made of) plus its routing (the operations that make it). Methods only work once the parent and all child items already exist — which is why they come after Step 2.

The Counterweight Hook's method is short: one material, three operations.

You import methods with two files, loaded in order:

BOM lines — one row per material, linking a parent readableId to a child readableId with a quantity. For the hook: parent CWH-A36-15, child A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240, qty 40 LB.

Routing operations — one row per operation, with its sequence, process (Flame Cut, Sandblasting, Inspection) and the work center it runs at, plus a time per piece.

Carbon stitches the two into the parent's method. From then on, any job for that part copies the recipe automatically.

Multi-level assemblies import the same way — just make sure every sub-assembly exists as an item first, and import the deepest level's BOM before its parents.

6 — Open orders

Last, bring in work still in flight: open quotes, sales orders awaiting fulfillment, and purchase orders awaiting receipt. By now everything they point at already exists.

How every importer works

Every importer follows the same five steps, so once you've done one, you've done them all.

Open the importer. Go to the module that owns the data — Items, Sales, Purchasing, Inventory — and click Import. Download Carbon's template CSV and pour your data into its columns.

Upload the file. Drop in your .csv. Carbon previews the first rows so you can confirm it parsed correctly.

Map columns. Point each of your columns at a Carbon field — your "Part No." to readableId, "UoM" to unitOfMeasureCode, "Make/Buy" to replenishmentSystem. If your headers match the template, mapping auto-fills.

Validate. Carbon checks every row before changing anything — flagging blanks, a missing UoM code, or a duplicate readableId. Fix flagged rows and re-upload.

Import. When the preview is clean, confirm. Carbon creates the records and shows a summary. Spot-check a few, then move to the next file.

Always run a small test batch first — five or ten rows — before importing thousands. A tiny run surfaces a wrong mapping or bad UoM code in seconds, not after you've created 5,000 mis-mapped parts.

Big or repeatable migration? Carbon is API-first — anything you can type in, you can script. For a large move or a recurring sync, scripting against the API beats hand-mapping CSVs. You can even have an AI assistant drive it through Carbon's built-in MCP server. See Using the API.
