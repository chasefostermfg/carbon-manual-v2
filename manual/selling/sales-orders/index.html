Sales orders

Capture demand and drive fulfillment from a sales order.

A sales order is your customer's commitment to buy: who's ordering, what, how many, when it's due, and at what price. Enter it once and Carbon carries it everywhere — the job that makes the part, the shipment that sends it, and the invoice that bills for it.

This page follows Acme's real order SO000069 — 500 Counterweight Hooks for Radiant Aerospace, $36,820, Make to Order.

Where this lives in Carbon

Sales orders live in the Sales module. Open Sales, then click Orders in the left sub-nav to see every order and its status.

The list shows each order's number, customer, total, and status at a glance. Click a row to open the order.

Two parts of an order

Every sales order has two parts:

  • A header — facts for the whole order: the customer, the order date, the payment terms, the ship-from location, and the customer's own PO number.
  • One or more lines — each line is one thing the customer is buying: an item, a quantity, a unit price, a promised date, and a fulfillment method.

Here's SO000069 open. The line CWH-A36-15 is 500 EA at $73.14. The tabs along the top — Shipments and Invoices — show what went out and what was billed.

Create a sales order

There are two ways an order is born. Converting a quote is by far the most common — it's how SO000069 was created.

When a customer accepts a quote, you don't re-type anything. Carbon copies the lines, quantities, prices, and the linked method, and keeps the two records linked.

Open the accepted quote. Go to Sales → Quotes and open it — for Acme that's Q000061. Confirm the line, quantity, and price.

Convert to Order. Click Convert to Order in the top-right. Carbon creates SO000069 and copies every line, including the Make to Order method on the hook line.

Review the header. Confirm the customer PO reference (55557777) and the ship-from location (Headquarters). Set the order date if it isn't today.

Not every order comes from a quote — a repeat customer might just email a PO.

Add a new order. In Sales → Orders, click the green Add button, top-right. Pick the customer (Radiant Aerospace). Carbon fills in their default terms, currency, and ship-to address.

Fill in the header. Set the order date, the ship-from location, and the customer's PO reference. Save to get an order number.

Add the lines. Add one line per item — covered next.

The customer PO reference (55557777) is the number your customer uses for the order. Capturing it prints it on the packing slip and invoice, so their accounts-payable team can match your bill without a phone call.

What's on a line

A line is one thing being sold. Open the Lines tab, pick the item, and Carbon pulls its description, unit, and a default price. Then you set quantity and the promised date.

FieldOn SO000069What it means
ItemCWH-A36-15The part, material, or service being sold.
Quantity500 EAHow many, in the item's unit (EA = Each).
Unit price$73.14What the customer pays per unit. Line total = qty × price.
FulfillmentMake to OrderHow Carbon delivers this line (see below).
Promised date2026-06-26The date you've committed to deliver by.

Pick a fulfillment method

The fulfillment method is the most important choice on a line. You set it per line, so one order can ship some items from stock and make others to order.

MethodUse it when…What Carbon does
Make to OrderThe item is built for this order.Spawns a job to make it.
Pull from StockYou keep the finished item on a shelf.Reserves on-hand inventory. No job.
Drop ShipA supplier ships straight to your customer.Links a PO that delivers to the customer.

SO000069's line is Make to Order — Acme doesn't stock 500 finished hooks, so each order is built fresh. That one setting spawns Job J000097 when the order is confirmed.

A line's default method comes from the item's replenishment setting: a Make part defaults to Make to Order, a Buy part to Pull from Stock. You can override it on any line.

Track shipments and invoices

The Shipments and Invoices buttons on the order are where fulfillment shows up. You don't fill them in by hand — they populate as goods leave and bills post.

  • Shipments — every time goods leave, a shipment record appears with the quantity and date. SO000069 has one shipment for all 500 hooks, sent 2026-06-11.
  • Invoices — when you bill, the invoice pulls its lines from what shipped. SO000069 was billed on AR000025 for $36,820 (goods plus $250 shipping) and marked Paid the same day.

Radiant Aerospace keeps coming back: the same hook runs on SO000070 (1,500 EA, in progress) and SO000071 (3,000 EA, queued). Each is its own order with its own job, but all point back to the same item and method.
