Suppliers

Set up the vendors you buy materials and services from.

A supplier (or vendor) is any company you buy from — a steel mill, a parts distributor, an anodizer. Add one before you can order from it. This page shows how, using Acme's real vendors.

Where this lives in Carbon

Suppliers live in the Purchasing module. Open Purchasing, then click Suppliers in the sub-nav across the top.

Set a supplier up once and the rest of buying gets easier. Every purchase order already knows who to email, where to ship from, how you pay, and the price to expect.

What a supplier record holds

You don't have to fill it all in at once — a name is enough to start. Add the rest as you go.

SectionWhat it holds
DetailsName, type, status, currency
ContactsThe people you email and call
LocationsShip-from / remit-to addresses
PaymentDefault payment terms
Supplier partsThe vendor's part numbers and prices for your items

Carbon gives every supplier a short ID like SUP000013. The friendly name shows everywhere; the ID keeps it stable even if you rename the company.

Acme's suppliers

A real shop keeps a mix of vendors. The Type column groups them so you can filter the list later.

Two small fields shape how a supplier behaves:

  • Type groups and filters the list. It doesn't affect pricing. Define your own types in Settings — Acme uses five.
  • Status controls whether you can buy now. Active shows everywhere; Inactive is hidden from new orders but kept for history — that's how you retire a vendor without deleting records.

Create a supplier

Let's add Ryerson, Acme's steel mill.

Open the form

In Purchasing → Suppliers, click New. Carbon opens a short form. The only required field is the Name.

Fill in the core details

Enter the Name, pick a Type, and confirm the Currency (Carbon defaults to your base currency — USD for Acme). Then click Save.

Carbon assigns the next ID (SUP000013) and opens the full record. The other tabs now unlock.

Add a contact

Open the Contacts tab and click New. Add a name, email, and phone for the person you deal with — usually a sales rep.

Mark one as the purchasing contact. That's who Carbon emails when you send a purchase order, so pick the person who takes orders. You can add as many contacts as you like.

Add a location

Open the Locations tab and click New to enter the ship-from or remit-to address. If a vendor ships from several plants, add one location per site.

Most vendors have a single location, so Carbon fills it in automatically on every purchase order. You only choose when there's more than one.

Set payment terms

Open the Payment tab and pick a default term. Net 30 means the invoice is due 30 days after it's issued. Setting a default here means every new order starts with the right terms.

Payment termWhat it means
CODPay when the goods arrive
Net 15Due 15 days after the invoice date
Net 30Due 30 days after the invoice date
Net 60Due 60 days after the invoice date
2% 10 Net 302% off if paid within 10 days, else due in 30
Net EOM 10thDue the 10th of the next month

Acme buys from Ryerson on Net 30 — the same terms that carry onto purchase order PO000057.

Link supplier part numbers

Your vendor rarely uses your part number. A supplier part links the two — it stores the vendor's part number and price for one of your items. The right number prints on the order and the price fills in automatically.

Add them two ways:

  • From the supplier → the Supplier Parts tab — good for loading a vendor's catalog.
  • From the item → the item's Suppliers tab — good for "who can sell me this?"

With this in place, picking Ryerson on a purchase order line for A36 plate auto-fills the $0.25/LB price Acme negotiated.

The finished supplier

Here's Ryerson once it's fully set up — the record the rest of buying leans on.

Now raising a purchase order is mostly point-and-click: pick Ryerson, pick the material, and Carbon already knows the contact, address, terms, and price.

Don't create a duplicate (a separate "Ryerson Inc." next to "Ryerson"). Duplicates split your spending across two records and make reports wrong. Search the list first and reuse the vendor if it exists.
