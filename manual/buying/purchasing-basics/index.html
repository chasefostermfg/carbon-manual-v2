Purchasing basics

Purchase orders, receiving and supplier quotes.

When a job needs material you don't have, you buy it with a purchase order (PO) — a document that tells a supplier what you want, how much, the price, and when. This page shows how to raise a PO, send it, and receive the goods onto your shelf.

We'll follow a real Acme example: PO000057 to Ryerson for the A36 Steel Plate that feeds job J000097.

Where this lives in Carbon

Purchasing is its own module. Open it from the left rail to see open POs, the amount on order, and the sub-nav for Purchase Orders, Suppliers, and supplier quotes.

Where this fits. A job (or low stock) creates a need → you raise a PO here → you receive the goods, which adds them to inventory → the job uses them → the supplier bills you. It's the buy-side mirror of the sell-side flow in Carbon in 15 minutes.

What's on a purchase order

A PO has two parts, shown as tabs on the record:

  • Header (Details tab) — the supplier, dates, deliver-to location, currency, and payment terms. One supplier per PO.
  • Lines (Lines tab) — one row per thing you're buying: an item, a quantity, a unit price, and a required date.

Get a price first. Not sure what something costs? Send a supplier quote (RFQ): you ask one or more suppliers for a price, then turn the best reply into a PO without re-typing. Carbon also remembers a supplier's last price and pre-fills it — Acme's A36 plate runs $0.25/LB at Ryerson.

Create the purchase order

Start a new order

In the Purchasing module, open Purchase Orders and click New Purchase Order. Carbon assigns the next number (Acme's was PO000057) and opens it in Draft — nothing is committed yet, so you can edit or delete freely.

Pick the supplier

Choose the supplier. Acme picks Ryerson. Carbon then fills in that supplier's currency (USD), payment terms (Net 30), and contact and address for you.

No supplier record yet? Create it first — see Suppliers. A PO must point at a real supplier.

Set dates and delivery

On the Details tab, confirm the order date (2026-06-12) and pick the deliver-to location. Acme ships to Headquarters. Leave Net 30 or override it for this order.

Add the line

On the Lines tab, click Add Line and set the type to Part. Search for the item — Acme picks A36 Steel Plate 1" (A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240). Then enter the three numbers:

  • Quantity — 54,001 LB
  • Unit price — $0.25 (Carbon pre-fills the last known price)
  • Required date — 2026-06-21

Carbon multiplies them into the extended price: 54,001 × $0.25 = $13,500.25.

Shortcut: buy from a job. Acme didn't type this line by hand. When job J000097 was planned, Carbon saw it needed 20,000 lb of A36 plate it didn't have and surfaced a shortage — from there you generate the PO with item, quantity, and supplier already filled in. See Jobs and Advanced purchasing.

Approve it and send it

A draft commits you to nothing. Two steps make it a real order.

Submit for approval

When the order is ready, submit it for approval. Carbon moves it from Draft to Needs Approval — large buys can need a manager's sign-off so nobody accidentally commits the company. Who approves what is set in Users & permissions.

A PO walks through these statuses:

StatusWhat it means
DraftBeing built. Editable, not committed.
Needs ApprovalSubmitted; waiting for sign-off.
To ReceiveSent; goods are on the way.
To InvoiceReceived; waiting on the supplier's bill.
CompletedReceived and invoiced in full. Closed.

Approve

An approver opens the order, checks the lines and total, and approves it. If something's wrong, they reject it back to Draft.

Send it

Once approved, click Send. Carbon builds a PO document (PDF) and emails it to Ryerson's contact. The order moves to To Receive. You can also download the PDF yourself.

Sending is not receiving. Sending tells the supplier what you want — it does not add anything to inventory. Material lands on your shelf only when you receive it.

Receive the goods

This is the step that actually changes your stock. When the truck arrives, create a receipt against the PO. Receiving:

  1. Adds the material to inventory at the deliver-to location.
  2. Records how much arrived per line, so Carbon knows what's still outstanding.
  3. Records the lot for batch-tracked items, for full traceability.

A36 Steel Plate is batch-tracked, so the receipt asks for a lot number — that's how Acme can later trace a hook back to the exact heat of steel it came from.

Start a receipt from the PO

Open PO000057 and click Receive (or start from Inventory → Receipts and pick the PO). Carbon pre-loads the open lines with the outstanding quantity — here the full 54,001 LB.

Confirm what arrived

Enter the received quantity per line. If they shipped everything, accept the suggested amount; if part, enter the smaller number and Carbon keeps the rest open. Pick the shelf / bin — Acme's A36 plate goes to R02-B01-01 at Headquarters.

Enter the batch (lot) number

Type the supplier's lot number from the mill certificate. This lot is now linked to the material, so Carbon records which lot the job consumes.

The lot shown (HEAT-A36-0612) is illustrative — enter whatever batch ID the mill certificate gives. Items that aren't batch- or serial-tracked skip this step.

Post the receipt

Click Post. Carbon then:

  • Increases on-hand stock at Headquarters by the received quantity.
  • Stamps the PO line as received and moves the order toward To Invoice.
  • Posts the inventory value to your books at the purchase cost.

The steel is now real, on the shelf, and ready for job J000097.

What happens next

Loading diagram...
  • The job consumes the plate, carrying the lot number into the finished hooks.
  • The supplier sends a bill, which you match to the PO and receipt as an accounts-payable invoice. Matching PO ↔ receipt ↔ bill is how you catch an overcharge before you pay.
