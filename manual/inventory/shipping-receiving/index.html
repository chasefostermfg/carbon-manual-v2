Shipping & receiving

Move goods in and out with shipments and receipts.

Goods cross your dock two ways:

  • In from a supplier — that's a receipt. It closes a purchase order line.
  • Out to a customer — that's a shipment. It closes a sales order line.

You never start from scratch. Each one points back to a document you already made.

Where this lives in Carbon

Both live in the Inventory module. Open it from the home screen, then use the sub-nav at the top to switch between Quantities, Receipts, and Shipments.

"Post" is the verb that matters. Until you post, a receipt or shipment is a draft you can edit or delete — nothing has moved. Posting is the commit: stock levels change and the source document updates. Get the numbers right before you post.

Receive against a purchase order

When a truck arrives with material you ordered, you create a receipt against the matching PO. Carbon already knows the part, quantity, and price — you're mostly confirming reality.

For the hero thread, Acme ordered 54,001 LB of A36 Steel Plate from Ryerson on PO000057.

Start the receipt

Go to Inventory → Receipts → New and pick PO000057 (or open the PO and choose Receive). Carbon copies the open lines onto a fresh Draft receipt.

The PO must be issued to the supplier first. You can't receive against a PO that's still a draft.

Confirm what actually arrived

Each line pre-fills with the quantity still outstanding. Edit the received quantity to match the packing slip:

  • Exact — all 54,001 LB came. Leave it as-is.
  • Short — receive what arrived; the PO line stays open for the rest.
  • Over — Carbon flags the overage so you can accept it or send it back.

Record the batch (if asked)

A36 Steel Plate is batch-tracked, so Carbon asks which heat of steel this is. Type the supplier's heat/lot number into the Batch field. This is what later lets you trace the steel that went into Radiant's hooks. Plain items don't show this field.

Tell Carbon where it goes

Each line needs a location and shelf (bin). Acme's steel lives at Headquarters in bin R02-B01-01 — its default — so the receipt suggests it for you. Change it if you're stocking it elsewhere.

Post the receipt

Choose Post. At that instant:

  • On-hand stock of A36 Steel Plate goes up in bin R02-B01-01.
  • The PO line moves toward Received / To Invoice.

The material is now real stock the job can pull from.

Ship against a sales order

Once parts are made and inspected, you send them. A shipment records what left the building, against the sales order. Job J000097 finished all 500 Counterweight Hooks; now they ship to Radiant Aerospace on SO000069.

Start the shipment

Go to Inventory → Shipments → New and pick SO000069 (or open the order and choose Ship). Carbon brings the order lines across as a Draft.

Pick the finished goods

Picking is choosing which stock fills the shipment. Carbon pulls the hooks from the job's finished-goods bin (8-1-H). Confirm the quantity per line — here, all 500 EA. For batch/serial items, you pick the exact lots here, which closes the traceability loop.

You can only ship what you have. Carbon won't let you ship more than is on hand. Short shipments are fine — the SO line stays open for the balance.

Choose a shipping method

The shipping method is the carrier the goods leave on. Acme has ten configured. A pallet of 500 steel hooks is freight, not a parcel — so pick LTL (Less Than Truckload) or FTL (Full Truckload), not UPS Ground.

The method is where a shipping cost rides along. SO000069 carries $250 of freight, which shows on the invoice but not in the goods subtotal.

Add tracking (optional)

If the carrier gives you a tracking number, add it here with package counts and weights. It travels with the record so anyone can answer "where is it?" later.

Post the shipment

Choose Post. A cascade fires:

  • The 500 hooks leave inventory from the picking bin.
  • The SO line shows 500 of 500 sent and moves toward Completed.
  • A shipment record is created — the link the invoice reads from next.

One number, entered once

You recorded one fact — "500 hooks left the dock" — and Carbon updated everything downstream. Acme can now raise sales invoice AR000025, built straight from what shipped (500 EA at $73.14, plus $250 freight), with no re-keying.
