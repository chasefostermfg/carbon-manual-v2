Defining an item

Parts, materials, tools and services — how to create and describe the things you make and buy.

Almost everything in Carbon starts with an item — a single, named thing you make, buy, sell, or use. Every quote, order, job and invoice points back to an item.

This page lives in the Items module. It covers the six kinds of item and how to create one.

Where this lives in Carbon

Items live in the Items module. Open it from the left rail, then pick a list: Parts, Materials, Tools, and so on. Each list has the same green Add button, top-right.

You describe an item once. After that, Carbon reuses the same record everywhere — so a price fix happens in one place.

The six item types

The first thing you choose is the type. It tells Carbon what role the item plays. Acme uses all six.

TypeWhat it isAcme example
PartSomething you make or sell as a finished goodCWH-A36-15
MaterialRaw stock you buy and consumeA36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240
ToolDurable shop equipment used across jobsTOL-CAL
ConsumableCheap supplies used up during workGRD-WHL
ServiceWork performed, not a physical goodOutside plating
FixtureA jig that holds a part during an operationFlame-cut fixture

You'll use Part and Material the most. They sit at opposite ends of the same job:

  • A Part is what you deliver. Acme's Counterweight Hook (CWH-A36-15) is a Part.
  • A Material is what you consume to make a Part. The hook is cut from A36 Steel Plate (A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240), which is a Material.

Tool vs Consumable is just value and durability. A Tool (a caliper, a torque wrench) lasts years and is worth tracking. A Consumable (sandpaper, welding wire) is cheap and used up. Ask: "would I notice if one walked off?" If yes, it's probably a Tool.

Create a Part

Let's create the Counterweight Hook the way Acme did. The flow is the same for any type — you just pick a different Type in step 2.

Open the Parts list

In the left rail, open the Items module and choose Parts. Click the green Add Part button, top-right.

Enter the basics

Fill in the four fields every item needs. Acme enters:

  • Type — Part
  • Part number — CWH-A36-15
  • Description — Counterweight Hook
  • Unit of measure — EA (Each)

The part number must be unique. Pick a readable scheme and stick to it.

Set tracking and Make-or-Buy

  • Tracking — Inventory (Carbon keeps a running on-hand count). Use Batch when the lot matters, Serial for high-value parts, Non-Inventory for services.
  • Replenishment — Make or Buy. The hook is Make: Carbon builds it on a job. The steel plate is Buy: Carbon orders it on a purchase order.

Tracking is hard to change once an item has history, so set it now. When unsure: Inventory for physical goods, Non-Inventory for services.

Save

Click Save. Carbon creates the item, marks it Active, and opens its detail page. That's the whole foundation.

Reading the item detail page

The detail page groups everything into tabs. The hook is a Make part, so its Manufacturing tab holds the method (the recipe) and a 3D model.

TabWhat it controlsFor the Counterweight Hook
DetailsNumber, description, type, UoM, trackingCWH-A36-15 · Part · EA · Inventory
ManufacturingThe method (BOM + routing) for a Make item40 LB A36 plate → Flame Cut → Sandblast → Inspect
PlanningReplenishment, reorder points, lead timeMake to Order
CostingUnit cost and how it's calculatedRolled up from the method
SalesSale price and customer part numbers$73.14 EA · Radiant part 730562
PurchasingSuppliers and prices (Buy items)n/a — this part is Made

Manufacturing and Purchasing are mirror images. A Make item fills in Manufacturing and leaves Purchasing empty; a Buy item is the reverse. The A36 steel plate fills in Purchasing (Ryerson, $0.25/LB) and has no method.
