Materials

Raw materials, forms, grades and dimensions for cut-to-size stock.

A material is the raw stock you buy and then cut, machine or form into parts — a steel plate, an aluminum billet, a length of brass bar. In Carbon, a material is a kind of item, described the way a metals distributor sells it: a substance, a form, a grade, and dimensions.

Where this lives in Carbon

Materials live in the Items module, on the Materials tab next to Parts. Open Items from the home screen, then switch to Materials to see your raw stock.

Material vs. part

Both are items, so they share the same fields — ID, unit of measure, costs, inventory, suppliers. They differ in what they describe and how you get them.

Use a material for anything you cut, machine, weld or form from raw stock. Not sure which item type to pick? See defining an item.

The four parts of a material

Every material is described by four attributes. Acme packs all four into a readable ID so the catalog reads at a glance.

  • Substance — the alloy. Carbon ships 17 built-in (Steel, Stainless Steel, Aluminum, Brass, Inconel, Titanium and more). Acme uses six, plus a custom one (Foam) for packaging.
  • Form — the shape it arrives in. Carbon has 21 built-in: Plate, Billet, Flat Bar, Round Bar, Hex Bar, Sheet, Tube, Angle, Channel and more.
  • Grade — the temper or spec, like T6 on aluminum or 304 vs 316 on stainless.
  • Dimensions — the physical size: plate thickness, sheet size, bar diameter.

Reading an Acme ID. A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240 = A36 (grade) · STEEL (substance) · PLT (form) · 1″ (thickness) · 96×240 (sheet, inches).

Most metal is bought in LB (pounds) — even when the part it feeds is counted in EA. Carbon mixes units happily: the Counterweight Hook consumes 40 LB of plate per 1 EA hook.

Cut-to-size stock and stock on hand

You rarely use a whole sheet. Each job pulls only the quantity it needs, in the material's unit:

  • The Counterweight Hook (CWH-A36-15) pulls 40 LB of A36 plate per hook.
  • A batch of 500 hooks needs 20,000 LB — not 500 whole sheets.
  • Carbon tracks the running pounds on hand and counts them down as you consume.

To see live stock, open the Inventory module. It lists on-hand quantity, reorder policy and replenishment for every item — including Acme's steel and aluminum.

Batch tracking and traceability

For materials, where the metal came from can matter as much as how much you have. A batch (also lot, or heat for steel) is stock that arrived together in one shipment. Acme's A36 plate has batch tracking on, so:

At receipt, Carbon records a batch number when steel arrives from Ryerson on PO000057. The pounds are tagged with that batch.

During the job, when plate is issued to job J000097, Carbon records which batch was used.

Forever after, each finished hook links back to that exact heat of steel — so if Ryerson reports a problem, Acme can answer "which orders shipped that steel?" in seconds.

This chain — shipment → batch → job → part → customer — is traceability. It's why Radiant Aerospace cares that Acme runs batch-tracked steel. More in tracking quality.

Turn on batch tracking before you receive any stock. Switching it on later leaves existing quantity with no batch number. For regulated work, enable it when you first create the material.

Create a material

Here's how Acme added the 1-inch A36 plate. Same steps for steel, stainless, aluminum or brass.

Start a new material. In the Items module, on the Materials tab, click the green Add button (top-right). Picking Material gives you the substance/form/grade fields.

Pick substance, form and grade. Choose Steel and Plate (PLT), enter grade A36, and dimensions 1″ thick, 96 × 240 sheet. Carbon builds a readable ID like A36-STEEL-PLT-1".96x240.

Set the unit of measure. Choose LB (Pound) — the unit you buy and consume the plate in.

Set replenishment to Buy. Acme buys plate, so set replenishment to Buy. This unlocks supplier and reorder settings.

Turn on batch tracking (if needed). For traceable stock, switch on Requires batch tracking now — before any inventory exists.

Add the supplier and save. On the Suppliers tab, add Ryerson at $0.25/LB, then Save. The material can now drop onto any method and onto a purchase order like PO000057.

Reuse, don't duplicate. Search first — Acme already stocks A36 plate in 1″, 2″, 3″ and 4″. Only create a new material when the physical stock is genuinely different.
